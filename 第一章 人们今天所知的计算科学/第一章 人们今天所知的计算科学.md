# 第一章 人们今天所知的计算科学

Chapter 1: Computing Science as we know today

计算机科学和计算机相关的程度，正如天文学和望远镜。

Computer science is no more about computers than astronomy is about telescopes - E. Dijkstra


数学诞生于第一个智人在兽骨上刻下划痕、或在绳结上打下绳扣的瞬间。
Math was born the exact moment the first Homo sapiens carved a notch on an animal bone or tied a knot on a string.

那一刻，人类意识首次完成了宇宙中最伟大的魔术：将“信息”与“物理实体”彻底解耦。那道划痕，就是人类历史上的第一个比特（Bit）。
At that moment, human consciousness accomplished the greatest magic in the universe: thoroughly decoupling "information" from "physical entities". That notch was the first Bit in human history.

随着文明的尺度放缩，天体运行的复杂性超越了裸脑的算力极限。对数表的编制，是在算法层面对算力进行的大规模“优化”。
As the scale of civilization zoomed, the complexity of celestial mechanics exceeded the computing limits of the naked brain. The compilation of logarithm tables was a massive "optimization" of computing power at the algorithmic level.

通过将乘除法简化成加减法，对数表拯救了天文学家的寿命。这也是“时间复杂度（Time Complexity）”概念的最初体现。
By simplifying multiplication and division into addition and subtraction, logarithm tables saved the lifespans of astronomers. This was also the initial manifestation of the "Time Complexity" concept.

在硅基芯片缺席的漫长岁月里，“计算机（Computer）”是一个人类职业，而非一台机器。
In the long eras absent of silicon chips, "Computer" was a human profession, rather than a machine.

从测绘星空到国家人口的大规模统计，人类通过组织成百上千个“碳基大脑”并行工作，完成了最早的分布式计算。
From mapping the starry sky to large-scale national population statistics, humanity accomplished the earliest distributed computing by organizing thousands of "carbon brains" working in parallel.

因此，今天我们所知的计算科学，并非关于如何制造更精密的硅晶体，而是关于如何用抽象的符号网络，去降维、压缩并预测这个复杂的宇宙。
Therefore, computing science as we know today is not about how to manufacture more precise silicon crystals, but about how to use abstract symbolic networks to dimension-reduce, compress, and predict this complex universe.
