# 第七章 图论与拓扑

Chapter 7: Graph Theory and Topology

前面六章充满了图，但没有命名它们。

The previous six chapters are filled with graphs, but none of them were named.

Lamport 的 happens-before 关系是一张有向无环图。文件系统的目录层级是一棵树。B 树的节点-指针结构是一棵树。程序的函数调用图是一张有向图。网络拓扑——从互联网骨干到 CPU 核间互联——全是图。**图不是"一个数学分支"。图是描述"两个事物之间是否存在某种关系"的通用语言。** 这一章的任务，是使这门语言显式化。

Lamport's happens-before relation is a directed acyclic graph. A filesystem's directory hierarchy is a tree. The node-pointer structure of a B-tree is a tree. A program's function call graph is a directed graph. Network topologies — from internet backbones to CPU interconnects — are all graphs. **A graph is not "a branch of mathematics." A graph is the universal language for describing whether a relationship exists between two things.** The task of this chapter is to make this language explicit.

---

## 树

## Trees

N 个节点的连通无环图，边的数量必然是 N−1。

A connected acyclic graph with N nodes necessarily has exactly N−1 edges.

再多一条边就形成环。再少一条边就不连通。**树是"刚好够"的连接——用最少的边数让所有节点可达彼此。** 这和信息论中的"最小描述长度"形成了对偶：如果你需要表示 N 个节点之间的依赖关系，而你能确定这些关系不会成环，那么你最少只需要 N−1 条有向边。树的边数，就是连通性的不可压缩下限。

One more edge and a cycle forms. One fewer and the graph disconnects. **A tree is "just enough" connection — the minimum number of edges to make all nodes mutually reachable.** This forms a duality with "minimum description length" in information theory: if you need to represent dependency relations among N nodes, and you can be certain the relations are acyclic, then you need at minimum N−1 directed edges. The edge count of a tree is the incompressible lower bound of connectivity.

这意味着：每一个比你当前看到的树"更小"的表示，必然会断开至少一对节点之间的连接。N−1 条边，**就是 N 个事物之间保持连通所需的最少关系**。任何声称能比这更少的表示——要么不是树，要么不连通。

This means: every representation "smaller" than the tree you see before you necessarily disconnects at least one pair of nodes. N−1 edges **is the minimum number of relations required to keep N things connected.** Any representation claiming to be smaller — is either not a tree, or not connected.

---

B 树的层级表（第六章）中，从根到任意叶子走过多少条边，就是该叶子在树中的深度。根是唯一没有父节点的节点，叶子是没有任何子节点的节点。内部节点——既有父又有子——是连接尺度之间的"路由器"。

In the B-tree hierarchy table (Chapter 6), the number of edges traversed from root to any leaf is that leaf's depth. The root is the only node with no parent; leaves are nodes with no children. Internal nodes — with both parent and children — are the "routers" connecting scales.

文件系统是同一棵树。一个目录是内部节点，一个文件是叶子。`/home/wsliu/ComputerScienceMinimum/第七章/` 是树中的一条路径——从根出发沿着边走到目标节点的序列。

A filesystem is the same tree. A directory is an internal node; a file is a leaf. `/home/wsliu/ComputerScienceMinimum/Chapter7/` is a path in the tree — a sequence of edges traversed from root to target node.

## 有向无环图

## Directed Acyclic Graphs

如果边有方向，但沿着方向走永远回不到起点——这就是**有向无环图**（DAG）。

If edges have direction, but following the direction can never return you to the starting point — that is a **directed acyclic graph** (DAG).

第五章的 Lamport happens-before 图（A→B 表示"A 发生在 B 之前"）是一张 DAG。如果出现过 A→B→C→A 的环，那就表示 A 发生在了自己之前——逻辑矛盾。算法的依赖拓扑是一张 DAG——如果 A 的输出是 B 的输入，那么 A→B。编译器就是在这张 DAG 上做拓扑排序来决定编译顺序。Git 的提交历史是一张 DAG——每个提交指向它的父提交，合并提交有两个父节点。如果出现环，Git 就无法线性化历史。

Chapter 5's Lamport happens-before graph (A→B means "A happened before B") is a DAG. If there were a cycle A→B→C→A, that would mean A happened before itself — a logical contradiction. A program's build dependency graph is a DAG — if A's output is B's input, then A→B. The compiler performs topological sort on this DAG to determine build order. Git's commit history is a DAG — each commit points to its parent, merge commits have two parents. If a cycle appeared, Git could not linearize the history.

DAG 是偏序的图表达。和全序（树或链表，每对节点都有先后关系）不同，偏序允许两个节点之间没有箭头——它们之间没有因果关系。这和 Lamport 的"两个并发的进程事件不一定要排序"完全一致。

A DAG is the graph representation of a partial order. Unlike a total order (a tree or linked list, where every pair of nodes has a before/after relation), a partial order allows two nodes to have no arrow between them — they have no causal relationship. This is exactly Lamport's "two concurrent process events need not be ordered."

## 遍历与局部性

## Traversal and Locality

同一张图，不同的遍历策略产生不同的访问序列——这些序列有不同的局部性轮廓（第六章）。

The same graph, under different traversal strategies, produces different access sequences — and these sequences have different locality profiles (Chapter 6).

DFS（深度优先）沿一条路径走到底再回溯。它的地址序列有很强的时间局部性——你刚处理完一个节点的所有子节点，它们的元数据大概率还在 L2 缓存里。BFS（广度优先）一层一层往下推，空间局部性好——同一层的兄弟节点在内存中可能相邻存放。

DFS (depth-first search) follows one path to its end, then backtracks. Its address sequence has strong temporal locality — you just finished processing all children of a node, and their metadata is likely still in the L2 cache. BFS (breadth-first search) pushes down level by level, with good spatial locality — siblings at the same level may be stored contiguously in memory.

遍历策略的选择，就是局部性轮廓的选择。垃圾回收器（GC）的标记阶段通常是 DFS 或 BFS；选择哪种，会直接影响在第十章提出的层级上产生的缓存缺失次数。

The choice of traversal strategy is the choice of locality profile. The mark phase of a garbage collector (GC) is typically DFS or BFS; which one is chosen directly affects the number of cache misses generated on the hierarchy described in Chapter 6.

## 欧拉公式与拓扑不变量

## Euler's Formula and Topological Invariants

画一个凸多面体——比如一个立方体——然后数它的顶点数 V，边数 E，面数 F。立方体：V=8, E=12, F=6。V − E + F = 2。

Draw a convex polyhedron — say, a cube — and count its vertices V, its edges E, its faces F. A cube: V=8, E=12, F=6. V − E + F = 2.

四面体：V=4, E=6, F=4。V − E + F = 2。

A tetrahedron: V=4, E=6, F=4. V − E + F = 2.

八面体：V=6, E=12, F=8。V − E + F = 2。

An octahedron: V=6, E=12, F=8. V − E + F = 2.

**V − E + F 永远等于 2，无论多面体的具体形状如何。** 这就是欧拉公式——它不依赖于一个形状的具体边长和面角，只依赖于它的"拓扑结构"。你可以把立方体的面压成球面，把它的边拉成曲线——只要你不撕裂也不粘贴任何东西，这个数字不变。

**V − E + F always equals 2, regardless of the polyhedron's specific shape.** This is Euler's formula — it depends not on the specific edge lengths or face angles of a shape, but solely on its "topological structure." You can squash the cube's faces onto a sphere, stretch its edges into curves — as long as you don't tear or paste anything, this number stays invariant.

拓扑，就是"在连续形变下保持不变的性质"的研究。一个甜甜圈和一只咖啡杯在拓扑上是同一个东西（它们都有一个洞），但它们和球面是不同拓扑的。球面上 V − E + F = 2；环面上 V − E + F = 0。这个差值——欧拉示性数——是拓扑不变量：无论你怎么在不撕裂的情况下扭曲曲面，它不变。

Topology is the study of "properties that remain invariant under continuous deformation." A donut and a coffee cup are topologically the same thing (both have one hole), but they are topologically distinct from a sphere. On a sphere, V − E + F = 2; on a torus, V − E + F = 0. This difference — the Euler characteristic — is a topological invariant: no matter how you distort the surface without tearing, it stays constant.

---

这和第二章的"结构即不可压缩性"有深层联系。一个随机比特串没有任何压缩规律——任何两段都是独立的。一个多面体在任何连续形变下保持 V − E + F = 2——**拓扑不变量就是系统中在"所有可能变形下"都不会被破坏的结构。** 它是不以"形"为转移的"质"。如果信息论中的"结构"是一段数据在压缩算法下的不变性，那么**拓扑就是几何形状在连续形变下的不变性。** 一个量化的是"符号的结构"，另一个量化的是"空间的形状"——但两者都在寻找"在这个东西所有可能的变化之下，有什么是不会变的"。

This has a deep connection to Chapter 2's "structure as incompressibility." A random bit string has no compression pattern — any two segments are independent. A polyhedron maintains V − E + F = 2 under any continuous deformation — **a topological invariant is the structure that cannot be destroyed under "all possible deformations" of the system.** It is the "essence" that does not depend on "form." If "structure" in information theory is the invariance of a piece of data under compression algorithms, then **topology is the invariance of geometric shapes under continuous deformation.** One quantifies the "structure of symbols"; the other quantifies the "shape of space" — but both seek the answer to: "under all possible changes to this thing, what stays the same?"

## 最大流 = 最小割

## Max Flow = Min Cut

一个网络流问题：你有源点 s（水厂），汇点 t（城市），中间有一张管道网络，每条管道有一个最大容量。你从 s 向 t 推流——最大能推多少？

A network flow problem: you have a source s (a water plant), a sink t (a city), and a network of pipes between them, each with a maximum capacity. You push flow from s to t — what is the maximum possible flow?

直觉上，答案被网络中"最窄的地方"限制——如果你能找到一组管道，把它们全部切断就断绝了 s 和 t 之间的所有联系，那么总流量不可能超过这组管的容量之和。

Intuitively, the answer is limited by the "narrowest point" in the network — if you can find a set of pipes whose total removal disconnects s from t, then the total flow cannot exceed the sum of their capacities.

这个直觉被最大流-最小割定理精确化了：**从 s 到 t 的最大可行流量，恰好等于 s 和 t 之间所有割集的最小总容量。**

This intuition is made precise by the Max-Flow Min-Cut Theorem: **the maximum feasible flow from s to t equals the minimum total capacity of any cut separating s from t.**

这和第五章的"同步受限于最慢的链路"同构，和第六章的"层级访问延迟受限于层级表中最慢的那一层"（DRAM 延迟是整条访问链的瓶颈）同构。**"最弱一环节决定系统全局吞吐"——这不只是一个经验法则，它在网络流的数学中有严格的证明。**

This is isomorphic to Chapter 5's "synchronization is limited by the slowest link," and Chapter 6's "hierarchy access latency is limited by the slowest level in the table" (DRAM latency is the bottleneck of the entire access chain). **"The weakest link determines global system throughput" — this is not merely a rule of thumb; it has a rigorous proof in the mathematics of network flow.**

## 小世界和所谓的"尺度无关/无标度"网络

## Small-World and the so-called "Scale-Free" Networks

扔 N 个节点在地上，每对节点之间以概率 p 连接一条边——得到的是随机网络（Erdős–Rényi 图）。节点度数近似泊松分布，没有"极度连接的枢纽"。

Throw N nodes on the ground, connect each pair with probability p — you get a random network (Erdős–Rényi graph). Node degrees follow an approximate Poisson distribution; there are no "extremely connected hubs."

但真实世界中的网络——网页的链接结构、社交关系、蛋白质相互作用、论文引用——既不是完全规则的（如网格），也不是完全随机的。它们有两个标志性特征：(1) 平均路径长度极短（"六度分隔"），(2) 度分布服从幂律——少数节点拥有海量连接，大多数节点只有极少连接。

But real-world networks — the link structure of the web, social relationships, protein interactions, paper citations — are neither perfectly regular (like a grid) nor perfectly random. They have two signature properties: (1) extremely short average path lengths ("six degrees of separation"), (2) degree distributions following power laws — a few nodes have massive numbers of connections, while most nodes have very few.

---

这类网络通常被称为"scale-free network"——但这个术语是糟糕的。幂律分布 P(k) ~ k^{-γ} 并不是"没有尺度"——它恰恰有一个鲜明的尺度：在对数坐标上，它呈现为一条具有固定斜率的直线。更准确的说法是**"对数尺度网络"**（log-scale network）：它不是在线性尺度上不变的，而是在对数尺度上自相似的。如果将互联网的节点数扩大 100 倍，枢纽节点的度数将按幂律比例扩大，而不是保持不变——这就是尺度。"Scale-free"这个词描述的不是网络的属性，而是幂律分布的一种数学特征，但这个特征被错误地搬进了名称里。

Such networks are commonly called "scale-free networks" — but this is a poor term. A power-law distribution P(k) ~ k^{-γ} is not "free of scale" — it has a very distinct scale: on logarithmic axes, it appears as a straight line with a fixed slope. A more accurate term would be **"log-scale network"**: it is not invariant under linear scaling, but self-similar on a logarithmic scale. If the number of nodes on the internet grew by 100×, the degree of the largest hub would grow proportionally according to the power law, not stay constant — that is a scale. The phrase "scale-free" describes not a property of the network, but a mathematical feature of the power-law distribution, which was mistakenly imported into the name.

---

这两个属性可以追溯到第四章的主题——**网络结构既不是纯随机的（最大熵），也不是纯规则的（最小熵），而是处在中间的某个尺度上。** 幂律分布本身就是一种结构：它比均匀分布有更低的熵（可压缩），但比完全规则的结构有更高的多样性（可适应）。现实网络的最优工作点——恰好在这条熵谱的中间某处。

Both properties trace back to the theme of Chapter 4 — **network structure is neither purely random (maximum entropy) nor purely regular (minimum entropy), but lies somewhere in between on the scale.** A power-law distribution is itself a kind of structure: it has lower entropy than a uniform distribution (compressible), yet higher diversity than a perfectly regular structure (adaptable). Real networks operate at an optimal point — somewhere in the middle of this entropy spectrum.

---

图是对关系的形式化。关系——谁和谁相连、谁依赖于谁、谁和谁是"之前/之后"——是计算科学中所有非孤立系统的共同语言。图不是可选的。它是去掉所有其他东西之后剩下的。

A graph is not optional. It is what remains after everything else is removed.
