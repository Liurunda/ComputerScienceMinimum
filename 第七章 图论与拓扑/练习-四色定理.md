# 练习 2 — 四色定理：从猜想到计算机证明

Exercise 2 — The Four Color Theorem: From Conjecture to Computer Proof

任何一张平面地图，用四种颜色就足以给所有国家着色，使得任何两个接壤的国家（非仅点接触）不同色。四种颜色，永远够。

Any planar map can be colored with at most four colors such that no two adjacent regions (sharing more than a point) have the same color. Four colors are always enough.

1852年由 Francis Guthrie 提出猜想。此后一个多世纪，无数数学家试图证明——均失败。1976年，Appel 和 Haken 用计算机检查了1936种"不可避免的构型"，首次完成证明。**这是人类数学史上第一个必须依赖计算机才能完成的证明。**

Conjectured by Francis Guthrie in 1852. For over a century, countless mathematicians attempted to prove it — all failed. In 1976, Appel and Haken used a computer to check 1,936 "unavoidable configurations," completing the first proof. **This was the first proof in mathematical history that necessarily relied on a computer.**

---

**(1)** 画一张用三种颜色"不够"的地图：至少需要四种颜色。

Draw a map where three colors are "not enough": at least four are required.

*提示：三个国家两两接壤不难画，四个国家两两接壤是否可能在平面上实现？Hint: three mutually adjacent regions are easy to draw on a plane; is four mutually adjacent regions possible on a plane?*

---

**(2)** 四色定理为什么只能用平面图来表述——甜甜圈表面需要几种颜色？

Why is the Four Color Theorem only stated for planar maps — how many colors does a torus (donut) surface require?

*提示：环面的欧拉示性数 ≠ 2。Hint: the Euler characteristic of a torus is not 2.*

---

**(3)** Kempe 在 1879 年发表了被广泛接受的"证明"，11 年后 Heawood 发现了一个反例。Kempe 的证明思路是什么？他错在哪里？

Kempe published a widely-accepted "proof" in 1879; 11 years later Heawood found a counterexample. What was Kempe's proof strategy, and where did it fail?

*提示：Kempe 链——交替着色路径。Hint: Kempe chains — alternating coloring paths.*

---

**(4)** Appel 和 Haken 的证明做了什么计算机能做但人不能做的事？为什么"检查不可避免构型集"必须是计算机？

What did Appel and Haken's proof do that a computer could do but a human could not? Why must "checking the unavoidable set of configurations" be done by computer?

*提示：1936 种构型，人手逐一验证需要一辈子。Hint: 1,936 configurations; manual verification would take a lifetime.*

---

**(5)** 你接受这个证明吗？如果一个定理的证明必须依赖计算机，但没有人能亲手验证每一步，它仍然是一个"证明"——还是更接近"实验"？

Do you accept this proof? If a theorem's proof necessarily depends on a computer, but no human can verify every step by hand — is it still a "proof," or is it closer to an "experiment"?
