**练习 1 — 设计一种用于 AI 编程的"受控自然语言"**

**Exercise 1 — Design a "Restricted Natural Language" for AI Programming**

本章结尾提出了一个核心命题：如果"好的自然语言用法能够被总结成一些规则"，我们就发明了一种新的"用于 AI 编程的语言"。这个练习就是让你亲手试一试。

The chapter ends with a core proposition: if "good natural language usage patterns can be summarized into a set of rules," we have invented a new kind of "language for AI programming." This exercise lets you try it yourself.

---

**(a) 收集好用法与坏用法**

**(a) Collect good usage and bad usage**

用一个支持自然语言编程的 AI 工具（如 Claude Code、Cursor、GitHub Copilot 等），完成以下三个编程任务各两次——一次用你直觉认为"好"的描述方式，一次用你直觉认为"差"的描述方式，记录两次生成代码的质量差异。

Use an AI tool that supports natural language programming (e.g., Claude Code, Cursor, GitHub Copilot, etc.). Complete each of the following three programming tasks twice — once using what you intuitively feel is a "good" description, once using what you feel is "bad." Record the quality difference between the two generated outputs.

三个任务（任选或全部）：
Three tasks (choose any or all):

- 实现一个具有特定行为的数据结构（如"一个支持 O(1) 查找最近插入的 100 个元素的缓存"）
- 为一个函数写完整的单元测试
- 重构一段已有的、可读性较差的代码

Implement a data structure with specific behavior (e.g., "a cache that supports O(1) lookup of the 100 most recently inserted elements"); Write complete unit tests for a function; Refactor an existing piece of poorly readable code.

对于每次尝试，记录：
For each attempt, record:

- 你的 prompt 原文
- AI 生成的代码是否一次就正确？如果不是，哪里出错了？
- 你如何修改 prompt 来修正错误？

Your original prompt text; Was the AI-generated code correct on the first try? If not, what went wrong?; How did you modify the prompt to fix the error?

---

**(b) 提炼规则**

**(b) Extract rules**

基于 (a) 中的实验，总结出至少 5 条"让 AI 生成好代码"的语言规则。每条规则应该是一个可操作的约束——是对自然语言用法的限制（restriction），而不是泛泛的建议。

Based on your experiments in (a), extract at least 5 language rules that "make AI generate good code." Each rule should be an actionable constraint — a restriction on natural language usage, not a vague suggestion.

示例（供参考，不要照抄）：
Example (for reference, do not copy):

- 规则 1：每个函数需求的描述必须以"给定...当...则..."的结构书写
- 规则 2：涉及数据结构的描述必须显式声明期望的时间复杂度

Rule 1: Every function requirement description must be written in the "Given... When... Then..." structure; Rule 2: Descriptions involving data structures must explicitly declare the expected time complexity.

对每条规则，解释它**约束了什么**（它防止了哪种自然语言的模糊性？）以及**牺牲了什么**（它让你不能说什么？）。

For each rule, explain what it constrains (what kind of natural language ambiguity does it prevent?) and what it sacrifices (what can you no longer say?).

---

**(c) 测试你的 CNL**

**(c) Test your CNL**

用你设计的那套规则（你的"受控自然语言"），重新完成 (a) 中的三个任务。这一次，严格遵守你自己制定的每一条规则。记录：

Using your set of rules (your "Restricted Natural Language" or CNL), redo the three tasks from (a). This time, strictly follow every rule you've established. Record:

- 与第一次自由描述相比，代码质量的提升（或下降）
- 是否出现了规则"不够用"的情况——你受限于规则无法精确表达某个需求？
- 是否有规则实际上阻碍了 AI 生成更好的代码？

Compared to the first free-form description, the improvement (or decline) in code quality; Were there situations where the rules were "insufficient" — you couldn't precisely express a requirement due to the constraints?; Were there rules that actually hindered the AI from generating better code?

---

**(d) 反思：CNL 的边界**

**(d) Reflection: the boundaries of CNL**

你的 CNL 必然在某些任务上表现好，在某些任务上表现差。回答：

Your CNL inevitably performs well on some tasks and poorly on others. Answer:

- 你的 CNL 最适合哪类编程任务？最不适合哪类？
- 如果将你的 CNL 与另一个同学的 CNL 做"互操作"——用你的 CNL 写的 prompt 放到ta的 AI 上执行——会出什么问题？这和你本章学到的"黑话"现象有什么关系？
- 你的 CNL 是有损压缩还是无损压缩？即：经过你的 CNL 约束后，是否有些合法的、值得实现的需求被"压掉了"？

What type of programming tasks is your CNL best suited for? Worst suited for?; If you "interoperate" your CNL with another student's CNL — running a prompt written in your CNL on their AI — what problems would arise? How does this relate to the "jargon" phenomenon discussed in this chapter?; Is your CNL lossy or lossless compression? That is, after being constrained by your CNL, are there legitimate, worthwhile requirements that have been "compressed away"?

**练习 2 — 黑话审计：你的小圈子语言压缩率**

**Exercise 2 — Jargon Audit: The Compression Ratio of Your In-Group Language**

---

**(a) 收集黑话**

**(a) Collect jargon**

列出至少 8 个你所属的某个小圈子（专业领域、兴趣社团、游戏社区、技术团队等）内部使用的"黑话"术语。每个术语标注：谁使用它？在什么场景下使用？

List at least 8 "jargon" terms used within a small circle you belong to (a professional field, hobby community, gaming community, tech team, etc.). For each term, note: who uses it? In what context?

---

**(b) 估算压缩率**

**(b) Estimate compression ratio**

对每个术语，估算它的"压缩率"：如果不使用这个术语，你需要用多少个字/词来解释同一个概念？将术语本身的字数除以外延解释的字数，得到一个近似的压缩比。

For each term, estimate its "compression ratio": if you couldn't use this term, how many words/characters would you need to explain the same concept? Divide the length of the term by the length of the expanded explanation to get an approximate compression ratio.

哪个术语的压缩率最高？它为什么能被压缩得这么厉害——是什么样的"共享上下文"使得如此简短的词汇可以承载如此复杂的含义？

Which term has the highest compression ratio? Why can it be compressed so aggressively — what kind of "shared context" allows such a short word to carry such complex meaning?

---

**(c) 入圈成本**

**(c) Cost of entry**

选两个压缩率最高的术语，分别回答：一个完全不了解这个圈子的人，需要掌握哪些前置知识，才能正确"解压"这个术语？

Choose the two terms with the highest compression ratios. For each, answer: what prerequisite knowledge would a complete outsider need to correctly "decompress" this term?

这些前置知识本身是否又依赖更多的黑话？如果是，描述这个依赖链——这反映了"入圈"的系统性成本。

Does this prerequisite knowledge itself depend on yet more jargon? If so, describe this dependency chain — this reflects the systemic cost of "entering the circle."

---

**(d) 当黑话失效**

**(d) When jargon fails**

回忆或设想一个场景：你的某个黑话术语被圈外人误解了。这个误解产生了什么后果？从信息论角度看，这次误解的本质是什么——是解码字典不匹配，还是同一个词汇在两个群体中指向了不同的语义空间位置？

Recall or imagine a scenario: one of your jargon terms was misunderstood by an outsider. What were the consequences? From an information-theoretic perspective, what is the nature of this misunderstanding — a mismatched decoding dictionary, or the same word pointing to different positions in semantic space for the two groups?

**练习 3 — 精度-模糊性光谱**

**Exercise 3 — The Precision-Ambiguity Spectrum**

---

**(a) 定位四个领域**

**(a) Locate four domains**

本章讨论了四个领域对语言精度的不同需求：医学（极高精度）、编程语言（高精度）、法律/外交（中等精度，保留妥协空间）、诗歌/电影（低精度，追求联想空间）。在"精度-模糊性"轴上为这四个领域标注位置，并各用一句话解释你如此定位的原因。

This chapter discusses four domains with different requirements for language precision: medicine (extreme precision), programming languages (high precision), law/diplomacy (moderate precision, preserving space for compromise), poetry/film (low precision, pursuing space for association). Mark the positions of these four domains on a "precision-ambiguity" axis, and explain your placement in one sentence each.

---

**(b) 寻找一个"中间地带"的例子**

**(b) Find a "middle ground" example**

找一个不属于以上四类、但在精度-模糊性上有独特取舍的领域（如食谱、乐谱、体育战术描述、游戏攻略等）。分析：这个领域的语言在哪些维度上精确？在哪些维度上刻意模糊？这些取舍是否合理？

Find a domain not among the four listed above, but one that makes a unique tradeoff on precision versus ambiguity (e.g., recipes, musical scores, sports tactics, game guides, etc.). Analyze: on which dimensions is this domain's language precise? On which dimensions is it deliberately vague? Are these tradeoffs justified?

---

**(c) 模糊性的正向价值**

**(c) The positive value of ambiguity**

本章声称"模糊性是联想、创造和幽默的来源，也是社会能妥协运转下去的前提"。选以下一个角度，用具体例子论证或反驳：

The chapter claims that "ambiguity is a source of association, creativity, and humor, and the precondition for society to compromise and function." Choose one of the following angles and defend or refute with concrete examples:

- 一个科学发现（或技术进步）的诞生，依赖于某个模糊概念的"可联想空间"
- 一个政治或社会妥协的成功，恰恰因为协议文本在关键处保持了模糊
- 一个笑话或文学作品的趣味，一旦被精确解释就消失了

A scientific discovery (or technological advance) whose birth depended on the "associative space" of a vague concept; A political or social compromise that succeeded precisely because the agreement text remained ambiguous at key points; A joke or literary work whose humor/charm disappears the moment it is explained precisely.

**练习 4 — 编译器的品味：语言设计者如何塑造代码质量**

**Exercise 4 — The Compiler's Taste: How Language Designers Shape Code Quality**

---

**(a) 品味的具体表现**

**(a) Concrete manifestations of taste**

本章指出："编译器将编程语言设计者的品味施加给了所有的普通程序员"。选两种你熟悉的编程语言（如 C 与 Python，或 Go 与 Rust，或 Java 与 JavaScript），对比它们的设计者通过编译器/解释器"强制"了哪些不同的品味。每种语言至少列出两点。

The chapter notes: "the compiler imposes the programming language designer's taste upon all ordinary programmers." Choose two programming languages you are familiar with (e.g., C vs. Python, Go vs. Rust, Java vs. JavaScript). Compare what different "tastes" their designers enforce through the compiler/interpreter. List at least two points for each language.

示例方向（不要照抄）：语法风格（缩进强制 vs 大括号自由）、类型检查的严格程度、错误处理的哲学（异常 vs 返回值）、内存管理的显式程度。

Example directions (do not copy): syntax style (forced indentation vs. free braces), strictness of type checking, error handling philosophy (exceptions vs. return values), explicitness of memory management.

---

**(b) 品味如何影响代码质量**

**(b) How taste affects code quality**

选 (a) 中一种语言的某个"品味约束"，分析它如何在实际工程中影响代码质量。考虑：这个约束是否减少了某种常见的 bug？它是否降低了代码的可读性或编写速度？综合来看，这个约束对"软件质量"的净效果是正还是负？

Choose one "taste constraint" from one of the languages in (a) and analyze how it affects code quality in practice. Consider: does this constraint reduce a common type of bug? Does it reduce code readability or writing speed? On balance, is the net effect of this constraint on "software quality" positive or negative?

**(c) 假如 LLM 成为编译器**

**(c) What if the LLM becomes the compiler**

在本章结尾的框架中，"受控自然语言"的规则相当于 LLM 时代的编译器——它将 CNL 设计者的品味施加给使用 AI 编程的人。基于你在练习 1 中设计的 CNL 规则，回答：

In the framework at the end of this chapter, the rules of a "Restricted Natural Language" serve as the compiler of the LLM era — imposing the CNL designer's taste on those who program with AI. Based on the CNL rules you designed in Exercise 1, answer:

- 你的 CNL 规则是否包含了某种"品味判断"？如果有，是什么品味？
- 如果你的 CNL 被广泛采用，它会如何塑造未来的代码质量？它可能消灭哪类问题？可能引入哪类新问题？
- 如果一个程序员觉得你的品味"不对"，ta 有哪些选择？这和传统编程语言中"换一种语言"的选择有什么异同？

Do your CNL rules contain some kind of "taste judgment"? If so, what taste?; If your CNL were widely adopted, how would it shape the quality of future code? What kinds of problems might it eliminate? What new ones might it introduce?; If a programmer finds your taste "wrong," what options do they have? How is this similar to or different from "switching to a different language" in traditional programming?
