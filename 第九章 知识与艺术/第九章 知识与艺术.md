# 第九章 知识与艺术

Chapter 9: Knowledge and Art

26 个英文字母，可以排列出大约 10^{42} 个长度为 30 的字符串。这个数字远超地球上的原子数。这个空间——所有可能的字母排列的总和——包含了莎士比亚全集和《独立宣言》，也包含了你在一周之内可能对朋友说出的每一句话和它们的每一个错误拼写版本。同样，所有的音乐，从贝多芬的交响曲到一段随机音符的序列，沉睡在 88 个钢琴键的排列组合之中，只是还没被按出来。

The 26 letters of the English alphabet can be arranged into roughly 10^{42} strings of length 30. That number far exceeds the atoms on Earth. This space — the totality of all possible letter arrangements — contains every line of Shakespeare and every line of the Declaration of Independence, as well as every thing you could say to a friend in a week and every misspelled version of it. Similarly, all music, from a Beethoven symphony to a random sequence of notes, lies dormant in the combinations of 88 piano keys — just not yet pressed.

---

所有的知识和艺术，都沉睡在人类已知的基本原语的组合空间里。

All knowledge and all art lie dormant in the combinatorial space of the basic primitives known to humanity.

"创造"不是凭空造出新的基本原语。创造是**剪枝**——从巨大的可能空间中找到一条通向有价值的结果的路径，这条路径上的每一个分岔口都淘汰了那些"错误的""低效的""丑陋的"分支。一个人写出了一篇好文章，不是因为他凭空增添了新的字母，而是因为他从 26 个字母的排列空间中成功地淘汰了所有不好的组合——最终在纸上留下来那个恰好有效的。一个人在实验室里验证了某个假设，不是因为他凭空增添了新的自然定律，而是因为他从所有可能的假设中，用一个实验剪掉了那些不符合观测的分支。

"Creation" is not conjuring new primitives out of nothing. Creation is **pruning** — finding a path through an enormous possibility space toward a valuable result, eliminating the "wrong," "inefficient," and "ugly" branches at every fork along the way. A person writing a good essay has not invented new letters out of thin air — they have successfully eliminated all the bad arrangements from the combinatorial space of 26 letters, leaving only the one that happens to work. A scientist confirming a hypothesis in the lab has not invented new laws of nature out of thin air — they have used an experiment to cut away, from among all possible hypotheses, every branch inconsistent with observation.

---

这个图景意味着："知识"和"信息"之间的关系不是平行的——它们是互逆的。

This picture implies: "knowledge" and "information" are not parallel concepts — they are inverses.

信息，如第二章所定义，是一段消息消除的不确定性——被消除的可能性越多，信息就越大。知识，在以信息论的语言重新表述后，就是：**一个人对某个领域中的可能性做了一个选择之后，该领域所剩下的那部分压缩。** 你学到的每一样东西——牛顿定律、一首歌的旋律、一个朋友的习惯——都剪掉了那个领域在你知道它之前的一个可能的分支结构。你不是"增加了"一些新的东西在你的脑子里，你是"排除了"一大堆一度好像还很可能的备选幻想。

Information, as defined in Chapter 2, is the uncertainty eliminated by a message — the more possibilities erased, the greater the information. Knowledge, reformulated in information-theoretic language, is this: **after a person has made a selection from among the possibilities in a domain, what remains is the compressed residue of that domain.** Everything you have ever learned — Newton's laws, the melody of a song, a friend's habits — has cut away a branch that might still have seemed possible before you knew it. You do not "add" something new to your mind. You "eliminate" a large set of alternatives that once looked plausible.

---

这个观点如果成立，反过来必须能用来解释"为什么我们有时候知道自己不知道的东西"以及"为什么有时候找不到答案"。

If this view holds, it must also work in reverse — it must explain why we sometimes "know what we don't know" and why sometimes we can't find an answer.

当你不知道怎么解一个数学题，对着空白的稿纸坐了半个小时——但你觉得你"应该能解出来"——那是因为你已经在可能性空间的某些区域见过类似的结构。在这个空间里，你还没有找到一条通往答案的路径，但你大概知道这条路应该在哪个区域。你已经在语义上压缩了一部分的可能性——而"压缩剩下的那一部分"正是搜索的感觉——往下，再往下，再剪掉一个分支……等等。这种"感觉到方向但还没到"的状态，当它持续了很长时间——就是"研究"。

When you don't know how to solve a math problem and stare at blank paper for half an hour — yet feel you "should be able to solve it" — it is because you have already seen similar structures in certain regions of the possibility space. You haven't found a path to the answer yet, but you roughly know which region the path should lie in. You have already compressed part of the possibility space — and "searching through the compressed remainder" is exactly what the feeling of searching feels like: down, further down, cut away one more branch... wait. This state — the sensation of direction without yet arrival, sustained over a long time — is "research."

又及——你在这个空间里剪来剪去，突然之间，你发现自己好像"突然开窍了"——搜索突然终止在一个你事先不知道的状态上。这可能就是"顿悟"的计算学解释——长期剪枝之后，路径连通到了一个新的分支区域，其内部结构在你意识到它之前就已经让你的内部世界模型更新了。拉马努金声称他的数学公式是在梦中被一位女神告诉他的。不管事实如何——他显然已经在事先进行了极为密集的长期剪枝。

And by the way — you keep cutting away in this space, and suddenly, you find yourself "getting it" — the search terminates abruptly at a state you didn't know beforehand. This may be the computational explanation of "insight" — after prolonged pruning, a path suddenly connects to a new branch region whose internal structure updates your internal world model before you are even consciously aware of it. Ramanujan claimed his mathematical formulas were told to him by a goddess in a dream. Whatever the facts — he had clearly done extremely intense, prolonged pruning beforehand.

---

回到"艺术"。如果创造是剪枝，那么"美"应该如何理解？

Back to "art." If creation is pruning, then how should "beauty" be understood?

一个人在巨大的可能性空间里找到了一个解，其周围的所有"相似的备选解"相比之下在某种直觉的意义上都让人感到冗长、别扭或错漏。那个被选出来的解——当你看到它的时候——你感到"这个是对的"。它不是任意的。它是这个局部山谷里的谷底。美感就是这个"谷底感"——在你独特的、已经压缩过的个人体验空间中，当大量可能的替代选项被剪枝之后，剩下来那一个恰好"不多不少"的选项的体验。

A person finds a solution in an enormous possibility space, such that all the "similar alternative solutions" surrounding it feel, under some intuitive criterion, verbose, awkward, or wrong by comparison. That chosen solution — when you see it — you feel "this is the one." It is not arbitrary. It is the valley floor in a local basin of that landscape. The aesthetic sense is this "valley-floor feeling" — the experience, within your uniquely compressed personal experience space, of the one solution that remains "just right" after a vast number of alternatives have been pruned away.

所以，好的审美——在音乐、写作、编程或者数学里——来自于你已经剪掉了多少。一个听了十年古典音乐的人和没听过的人，在听到一个新乐句的时候，激发的"可能后续"的空间维度完全不同。一个人的个人体验空间，并不是一个空旷的广场——它是一个已经删除了大量丑陋分支的大型地下编辑室。审美经验不是一个神秘的、无法分析的"第六感"。它是你的大脑在几秒之内自发完成的排除计算——只不过它发生得太快、太深，让你以为你"天生就知道"。

Thus, good taste — in music, writing, programming, or mathematics — comes from how much you have already pruned. A person who has listened to classical music for ten years and a person who hasn't, upon hearing a new phrase, activate completely different dimensionalities in the space of "possible continuations." A person's private experiential space is not an empty plaza — it is a vast underground editing room from which an enormous number of ugly branches have already been deleted. The aesthetic experience is not a mysterious, unanalyzable "sixth sense." It is an elimination computation that your brain performs spontaneously within seconds — it just happens too fast, too deep, making you think you "just knew."

---

这个图景并没有让艺术失去魔力。它只是说：魔力不在于结果本身，而在于你之前走的那么远的路。当你对一个新认识的人说："我喜欢这首歌"，你其实在说："从我听过的几万首歌的体验来看，在这个特定的可能性空间里，这个分支是我选择留下来的"。那不是"虚无"。这正是艺术哲学的"存在先在本质"的具体体现——你先活过了一个能删掉很多错误的、丑陋的、低效的意见的生活，然后你才能在另一个人的作品面前认出那确实是好东西。

This picture does not rob art of its magic. It only says: the magic is not in the result itself, but in how far you walked to get there. When you tell someone you've just met, "I like this song," you are actually saying: "From my experience of tens of thousands of songs I've heard, within this particular possibility space, this branch is the one I chose to keep." That is not "nothing." It is the concrete manifestation of the existentialist principle "existence precedes essence" — you first lived a life that could delete many wrong, ugly, inefficient opinions, and only then could you stand before another person's work and recognize that it is indeed the real thing.

---

## 本章小结

## Chapter Summary

知识不是增加。知识是剪枝——在一个巨大的可能性空间中淘汰错误、低效和丑陋的分支。Knowledge is not addition. Knowledge is pruning — eliminating wrong, inefficient, and ugly branches within an enormous possibility space.

艺术不是凭空创造。艺术是在已剪枝的个人经验空间里辨认出那个"不多不少"的解——并因此感到"这个是对的"。Art is not creation from nothing. Art is recognizing, within a pruned personal experiential space, the solution that is "just right" — and feeling, therefore, "this is the one."

这两件事在计算的框架中是同一件事——压缩。你学到的东西是你成功压缩掉的可能性。你认为美好的东西是你已压缩过的经验空间里留下的结构。知识和艺术，都是高度压缩的、个人剪枝后的"剩余"。They are, in a computational framework, the same thing — compression. What you have learned is the possibilities you have successfully compressed away. What you find beautiful is the structure left behind in your already-compressed experiential space. Knowledge and art are both the highly compressed "residue" after personal pruning.
