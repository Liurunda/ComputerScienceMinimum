# 练习 1 — 精读《诗云》《春江花月夜》《天问》

Exercise 1 — A Close Reading of "The Poetry Cloud," "Spring River Flower Moon Night," and "Heavenly Questions"

本章的核心命题：知识是剪枝，艺术是在被剪枝过的可能空间中找到那个"不多不少"的解。

This chapter's core proposition: knowledge is pruning; art is finding the solution that is "just right" within a pruned possibility space.

以下三篇作品，从三个完全不同的角度触碰了同一个问题——"人类的知识和艺术，在这个巨大的可能性空间中，究竟是怎么回事？" 请按顺序精读并回答。

Three works below touch, from three completely different angles, the same question — "What, exactly, are human knowledge and art doing within this enormous possibility space?" Read them in sequence and answer.

---

## 刘慈欣《诗云》

Liu Cixin, "The Poetry Cloud"

一篇科幻小说。一个掌握终极技术的外星文明试图用穷举法生成所有可能的中国古典格律诗——将汉字的全部合法排列统统计算并存储。故事追问：当"所有可能的诗"都被枚举出来之后，"创作"还剩什么？

A science fiction story. An alien civilization possessing ultimate technology attempts to generate all possible classical Chinese regulated verse through brute-force enumeration — computing and storing every legal arrangement of Chinese characters. The story asks: when "all possible poems" have been enumerated, what is left of "creation"?

---

**(1)** 外星文明在生成"所有可能的诗"时，假设了"好的诗"可以被预先定义（如平仄、押韵、字数）。那么，穷举式地产生所有的诗——"好的诗"是否必然会被包含在穷举集合中？

The alien civilization assumes that a "good poem" can be defined in advance (e.g., tonal patterns, rhyme, character count). If all poems are generated by brute force — will "good poems" necessarily be included in the brute-force set?

---

**(2)** 在本章的框架中，"所有可能的诗"是一个可能性空间，而"写出了一首具体的优美诗歌"是该空间中的一次剪枝。读完《诗云》后，请用自己的话回答：如果在技术压倒一切的图景下——在小说中——某一个人发现了一首绝美的诗，ta 是一个"创作者"还是"发现者"？

Within this chapter's framework, "all possible poems" is a possibility space, and "writing a specific beautiful poem" is one pruning act within that space. After reading "The Poetry Cloud," answer in your own words: if someone discovers a stunningly beautiful poem within this brute-force set — is that person a "creator" or a "discoverer"?

---

## 张若虚《春江花月夜》

Zhang Ruoxu, "Spring River Flower Moon Night"

一首唐代七言诗，被闻一多誉为"诗中的诗，顶峰上的顶峰"。全诗三十六句，四句一韵，回环往复，将春、江、花、月、夜五种意象交织成一个流动的整体。

A Tang Dynasty poem in seven-character lines, praised by Wen Yiduo as "the poem of poems, the peak of peaks." Thirty-six lines, shifting rhyme every four lines, weaving spring, river, flowers, moon, and night into a single flowing whole.

---

**(3)** 试着指出，如果这首诗是一棵被剪枝过的分支（它来自于那个"所有可能的唐人七言诗"的空间），你觉得它剪掉了哪两三种"唐人可能写过但没有这么写"的东西？（它没有怎么写？它拒绝走什么捷径？）

Think about: if this poem is a particular pruned branch from the space of "all possible Tang seven-character poems," what two or three things did it prune away — things that Tang poets *could* have written but *didn't* in this particular poem? (What did it refuse to write? What shortcut did it refuse to take?)

---

**(4)** 读完这首诗后，你能指出哪些"冗余"被它淘汰了——它不给出什么答案，但却让你觉得"这样就够了"？用第八章"谷底感"的概念描述你的阅读体验。

After reading this poem, can you point out what "redundancy" it eliminated — what answers it does not give, yet leaves you feeling "this is enough"? Use Chapter 8's concept of the "valley-floor feeling" to describe your reading experience.

---

## 屈原《天问》

Qu Yuan, "Heavenly Questions"

一首连续追问了一百七十多个问题的诗——从宇宙的起源问到历史的公道。这些问题在古代没有人知道答案，在今日的读者也未必知道答案。

A poem that asks over 170 consecutive questions — from the origin of the cosmos to the justice of history. No one in antiquity knew the answers; readers today may not know them either.

---

**(5)** 在本章的语言下，屈原的"一百七十多个提问"可以被视为一种在巨大的可能性空间中**尚未完成**的剪枝。他问的这些问题，为什么不直接用陈述句？提问（而不是陈述）如何从另一个角度"裁剪"了现实的表征？**

In the language of this chapter, Qu Yuan's "170-plus questions" can be seen as a pruning in progress — not yet completed — within an enormous possibility space. Why did he not use declarative sentences? How does questioning (rather than stating) "cut away" representations of reality from a different angle?

---

**(6)** 综合题。将这三篇作品放在一起：《诗云》是一台机器穷举了所有可能的诗，《春江花月夜》是一个人写出了其中一首特殊的诗，《天问》是一个人连续问了一百多个世界问题。用本章的"剪枝"框架解释它们之间的关系。

Synthesis. Consider these three works together: "The Poetry Cloud" is a machine brute-forcing all possible poems; "Spring River Flower Moon Night" is one person writing one specific poem from that space; "Heavenly Questions" is one person asking 170 consecutive questions about the world. Use this chapter's "pruning" framework to explain the relationship among them.
