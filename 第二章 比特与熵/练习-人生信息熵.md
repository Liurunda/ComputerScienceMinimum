# 练习 6 — 一个人一生的信息熵

Exercise 6 — The Information Entropy of a Human Life

以下是对一个活到 80 岁的现代人的基本假设（所有数字均可按你的常识修正，但必须在计算中显式声明）：

The following are basic assumptions about a modern human who lives to age 80 (you may adjust any number based on your own knowledge, but must explicitly declare your assumptions):

- 基因组：约 3.2 × 10^9 个碱基对（Genome: ~3.2 × 10^9 base pairs）
- 大脑皮层突触数：约 1.5 × 10^14（Cortical synapses: ~1.5 × 10^14）
- 平均体重 70 kg（Average body mass: 70 kg）
- 全天感官输入带宽（视+听+触+嗅+味）：约 10^7 bps（Sensory bandwidth: ~10^7 bps）

---

**(a) 遗传层：最底层的引导程序**

**(a) The Genetic Layer: The Bootstrap Program**

每个碱基对有 4 种可能（A/T/C/G），纯信息论编码为 2 比特。

Each base pair has 4 possibilities (A/T/C/G), encoding 2 bits in pure information-theoretic terms.

(i) 人类基因组的原始信息量是多少比特？换算成 MB 是多少？

(i) What is the raw information content of the human genome in bits? Convert to MB.

(ii) 但基因组中约 98.5% 的序列不编码蛋白质——它们包含调控区、结构重复区、转座子、以及功能尚不明确的序列。从"制造一个人类的说明书"的角度，**基因组的"有效信息熵"是否一定远小于 2 比特/碱基对？** 论证你的答案。谁来决定什么是"有效"——是自然选择、是发育生物学家、还是这套信息后来被读出的方式？

(ii) However, ~98.5% of the genome does not code for proteins — it contains regulatory regions, structural repeats, transposons, and sequences of unknown function. From the perspective of "instructions to build a human," **is the genome's "effective information entropy" necessarily much less than 2 bits per base pair?** Justify your answer. Who decides what counts as "effective" — natural selection, the developmental biologist, or the way this information is later read out?

(iii) 你的基因组与任何一个随机陌生人的基因组约 99.9% 相同。仅从"差异信息"的角度看，描述"你与所有其他人的不同之处"，大约需要多少比特？

(iii) Your genome is ~99.9% identical to any random stranger's. From the perspective of "differential information" alone, approximately how many bits are needed to describe "what makes you different from all other humans"?

---

**(b) 感官层：一生的输入带宽**

**(b) The Sensory Layer: A Lifetime of Input Bandwidth**

(i) 假设一个清醒的人全天感官输入总带宽约为 10^7 bps（视觉主导）。清醒时间按每天 16 小时、每年 365 天、共 80 年计算。**一生中进入感官系统的原始信息总量**是多少比特？将这个数字与 (a)(i) 的基因组信息量对比——它们相差几个数量级？

(i) Suppose a waking person's total sensory input bandwidth is ~10^7 bps (vision-dominant). Assume 16 waking hours/day, 365 days/year, for 80 years. **What is the total raw information entering the sensory system over a lifetime** in bits? Compare this with the genomic information from (a)(i) — how many orders of magnitude apart are they?

(ii) 但这个数字几乎毫无意义——因为感官信息的压倒性部分在一秒之内就被丢弃。大脑在每一个瞬间都在执行「熵作为尺度放缩的度量」一节所说的**"向上爬"**：将高维的感官流压缩为低维的知觉对象和语义结构。如果你必须为这个过程估算一个"终生压缩比"（感官输入总量 vs. 最终存入长期记忆的信息量），你会怎么估算？给出你的推理，即使推理充满了猜测。

(ii) Yet this number is almost meaningless — because the overwhelming majority of sensory information is discarded within a second. At every moment, the brain is performing what the「熵作为尺度放缩的度量」section called **"climbing upward"**: compressing the high-dimensional sensory stream into low-dimensional perceptual objects and semantic structures. If you had to estimate a "lifetime compression ratio" (total sensory input vs. what ultimately enters long-term memory), how would you estimate it? Give your reasoning, even if it is full of guesswork.

---

**(c) 存储层：记忆与知识**

**(c) The Storage Layer: Memory and Knowledge**

(i) 神经科学估计大脑皮层约有 1.5 × 10^14 个突触。如果每个突触的强度可被视为约 4.7 比特的信息（基于突触强度的可分辨等级数的一种估计），大脑皮层可存储的信息上限约为多少比特？与 (b)(i) 中一生的感官输入总量相比，这个"存储上限"够用吗？还是暗示大脑必须持续地、激进地压缩？

(i) Neuroscience estimates the cerebral cortex has ~1.5 × 10^14 synapses. If each synapse's strength can be treated as encoding ~4.7 bits of information (one common estimate based on distinguishable strength levels), what is the upper bound of the cortex's information storage in bits? Compared to the lifetime sensory input from (b)(i), is this "storage ceiling" sufficient — or does it imply that the brain must compress continuously and aggressively?

(ii) 但人类记忆不是硬盘。它不是精确写入、永不变质的。记忆在每次回忆时会被**重新构建**——这意味着"读取"本身就会改变存储的内容。在「比特与熵」这一章的框架中，记忆的这种"读取即改写"性质，与兰道尔原理中的"擦除即排热"之间，是否存在结构上的类比？请讨论。

(ii) But human memory is not a hard drive. It is not written precisely and does not persist unchanged. Memories are **reconstructed** each time they are recalled — meaning the act of "reading" itself alters the stored content. Within the framework of this chapter, is there a structural analogy between this "read-rewrite" nature of memory and Landauer's "erasure dissipates heat"? Discuss.

---

**(d) 输出层：一生说了什么、创造了什么**

**(d) The Output Layer: What Was Said and Created in a Lifetime**

(i) 假设此人在一生中平均每天说 8,000 个词（中文），每个词的信息熵（在上下文中）约为 5 比特。此外，此人在一生中写了约 10^7 个词的文本（邮件、笔记、社交媒体等）。**估算此人一生的语言输出总信息熵**。与 (b) 的感官输入相比，输出大约是输入的几分之一？

(i) Suppose this person speaks an average of 8,000 words per day (in Chinese), each word carrying ~5 bits of information entropy in context. Additionally, the person writes ~10^7 words of text over a lifetime (emails, notes, social media, etc.). **Estimate the total information entropy of this person's linguistic output over a lifetime.** Compared to the sensory input from (b), what fraction is output relative to input?

(ii) （开放题）一个人的"输出/输入信息比"是否可以被视为某种**信息代谢率**——类似于生物体将食物转化为做功的热力学效率？这个比值是否反映了一个人"处理信息的深度"？为什么不能或为什么能？

(ii) (Open) Can a person's "output-to-input information ratio" be viewed as a kind of **information metabolic rate** — analogous to an organism's thermodynamic efficiency in converting food into work? Does this ratio reflect the "depth of information processing" in a person? Why or why not?

**(e) 物理层：身体作为热力学对象**

**(e) The Physical Layer: The Body as a Thermodynamic Object**

人体约 70 kg，主要成分为水、蛋白质和脂肪。一个人的身体，作为一个热力学系统，包含着巨大的物理熵。

The human body is ~70 kg, composed mainly of water, protein, and fat. A person's body, as a thermodynamic system, contains enormous physical entropy.

(i) 近似估算一个活人身体的热力学熵（以比特等价单位计）。可以参考：室温下凝聚态物质每个原子的熵约为 1–10 k。这个数字与 (c)(i) 中大脑皮层存储的信息量相差几个数量级？

(i) Approximate the thermodynamic entropy of a living human body (in bit-equivalent units). Reference: the entropy per atom in condensed matter at room temperature is roughly 1–10 k. How many orders of magnitude separate this from the information stored in the cerebral cortex from (c)(i)?

(ii) 人死后，身体不再维持稳态——体温下降、结构瓦解、微生物分解将组织转化为更简单的化合物。从热力学角度看，**死亡是一个熵增事件还是一个熵减事件？** 从信息论角度看呢？这两个回答是否矛盾？

(ii) After death, the body ceases to maintain homeostasis — temperature drops, structures break down, microbial decomposition converts tissues into simpler compounds. From a thermodynamic perspective, **is death an entropy-increasing or entropy-decreasing event?** What about from an information-theoretic perspective? Are the two answers in conflict?

---

**(f) 外化层：被他人存储的信息**

**(f) The Externalized Layer: Information Stored in Others**

一个人一生中不仅在自己体内存储和处理信息——他还将信息写入他人的记忆、数据库、档案系统和数字平台。你出生时，全球数字数据总量约为你成年时的千分之一；你死的时候，关于你的数据分布在从社保系统到朋友圈的数百个异质存储介质中。

A person not only stores and processes information within their own body — they also write information into others' memories, databases, archival systems, and digital platforms. At birth, the global digital data volume was perhaps a thousandth of what it is in adulthood; at death, data about you is distributed across hundreds of heterogeneous storage media, from social security systems to friend circles.

(i) 估算此人一生中由他人（朋友、家人、同事）形成的关于此人的"外部记忆"的近似信息熵。这些外部记忆加在一起，可能比此人自身的大脑存储量更大还是更小？

(i) Estimate the approximate information entropy of the "external memories" formed by others (friends, family, colleagues) about this person. Could the sum of these external memories be larger or smaller than the person's own brain storage?

(ii) （开放题）博尔赫斯写过："一个人死的时候，死的是他眼中最后一个见过的人。"从信息论的角度解读这句话：**一个人的"信息存在"持续到最后一个携带关于他/她的信息的比特被擦除为止。** 这个"信息寿命"与生物学寿命之间，是分离的。估算一下你自己的"信息半衰期"——关于你的信息在你去世后，大约需要多久才会衰减到一半？

(ii) (Open) Borges wrote: "When a man dies, the last person he has seen dies with him." Interpret this from an information-theoretic perspective: **a person's "information existence" persists until the last bit carrying information about them is erased.** This "information lifespan" is decoupled from biological lifespan. Estimate your own "information half-life" — roughly how long after your death would the information about you decay to half its current volume?

**(g) 综合题：人的熵预算**

**(g) Synthesis: The Entropy Budget of a Human**

回顾你在 (a)–(f) 中的所有估算。现在绘制一个人一生的**"熵预算"曲线**：

Review all your estimates from (a)–(f). Now sketch the **"entropy budget"** curve of a human life:

- 横轴为时间（从受孕到死后100年）
- 竖轴为信息量（对数尺度，比特）
- 在同一个坐标系中画出至少四条曲线：
  1. 遗传信息量（从受孕到死亡，恒定？还是变化？）
  2. 体内存储的信息量（大脑记忆 + 技能知识，从零开始，在衰老阶段可能下降）
  3. 外化信息量（在他人和外部系统中的记录，从零开始，持续增长或衰减）
  4. 累计感官输入量（单调增长）

The x-axis represents time (from conception to 100 years after death). The y-axis represents information quantity (log scale, bits). On the same coordinate system, plot at least four curves:
1. Genetic information (from conception to death — constant, or changing?)
2. Information stored within the body (brain memory + skills, starting from zero, possibly declining in old age)
3. Externalized information (records in others and external systems, starting from zero, growing or decaying)
4. Cumulative sensory input (monotonically increasing)

然后回答：**这四条曲线中，哪一条最接近"一个人的本质"？** 如果让你只保留一条曲线来代表这个人——你会选哪条？为什么？

Then answer: **which of these four curves comes closest to "the essence of a person"?** If you had to keep only one curve to represent this person — which would you choose, and why?
