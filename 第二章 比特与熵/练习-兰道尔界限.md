# 练习 2 — 兰道尔界限与数据中心的热力学账单

Exercise 2 — Landauer's Bound and the Thermodynamic Bill of a Data Center

一个云数据中心每秒擦除 10^18 个比特（1 exabit/s）。假设环境温度 T = 300K，玻尔兹曼常数 k ≈ 1.38 × 10^{-23} J/K。

A cloud data center erases 10^18 bits per second (1 exabit/s). Assume ambient temperature T = 300K, Boltzmann constant k ≈ 1.38 × 10^{-23} J/K.

---

**(a)** 根据兰道尔原理，这个数据中心每秒必须至少向环境排放多少热量（以瓦特计）？

**(a)** According to Landauer's Principle, what is the minimum heat this data center must dump into the environment per second (in watts)?

---

**(b)** 现实中，一个典型数据中心每比特擦除实际耗能约为 10^{-12} J（远超兰道尔界限）。这个"工程现实"与"物理下限"之间的差距——约几个数量级——是什么造成的？未来计算是否可能逼近兰道尔界限？

**(b)** In reality, a typical data center consumes about 10^{-12} J per bit erased (far above Landauer's bound). What accounts for this gap of several orders of magnitude between "engineering reality" and the "physical lower bound"? Could future computation approach Landauer's bound?

---

**(c)** （思考题）如果一个计算过程完全可逆（reversible computing），从理论上讲，它是否还需要排热？读一读 Charles Bennett 1973 年的论文《Logical Reversibility of Computation》，写一段不超过200字的笔记。

**(c)** (Thought question) If a computation is fully reversible (reversible computing), does it theoretically need to dissipate heat at all? Read Charles Bennett's 1973 paper "Logical Reversibility of Computation" and write a note of no more than 200 words.
