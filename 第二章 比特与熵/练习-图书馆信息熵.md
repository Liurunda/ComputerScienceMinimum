# 练习 1 — 一座图书馆的信息熵

Exercise 1 — The Information Entropy of a Library

以下是一座典型大学图书馆的基本数据（所有数字均可按你的常识修正，但必须在计算中显式声明）：

Below are the basic parameters of a typical university library (you may adjust any number based on your own common sense, but must explicitly declare your assumptions in the calculation):

- 藏书量：10^6 册（Volumes: 10^6）
- 每册平均 300 页，每页约 500 个汉字（Average 300 pages/volume, ~500 Chinese characters/page）
- 建筑质量：~10^7 kg，约含 10^33 个原子（Building mass: ~10^7 kg, containing roughly 10^33 atoms）
- 假设每个汉字在连续文本中实际携带的熵约为 2 比特（Assume each Chinese character carries ~2 bits of actual information entropy in running text — you may research or choose another value, but must justify it）

---

**(a) 文本层的香农熵**

**(a) Shannon Entropy at the Text Layer**

估算全部藏书的文本内容所携带的信息熵（以比特计）。这是整座图书馆最显眼的信息层——但远非唯一的信息层。

Estimate the information entropy carried by the textual content of the entire collection (in bits). This is the most visible information layer of the library — but far from the only one.

---

**(b) 物理层的热力学熵**

**(b) Thermodynamic Entropy at the Physical Layer**

图书馆的纸张总质量约为 10^6 kg（每册书约 1 kg）。将这些纸张近似为纤维素 (C₆H₁₀O₅)ₙ，估算全部纸张在常温（T = 300K）下的热力学熵的数量级（以比特等价单位计，即除以 k ln 2）。

The total paper mass is approximately 10^6 kg (~1 kg per volume). Approximating the paper as cellulose (C₆H₁₀O₅)ₙ, estimate the order of magnitude of the thermodynamic entropy of all the paper at room temperature (T = 300K), expressed in bit-equivalent units (i.e., divided by k ln 2).

*提示：一个原子在凝聚态物质中的热力学熵约为 1–10 k。不需要精确计算——你需要的是数量级比较。*

*Hint: the thermodynamic entropy per atom in condensed matter is roughly 1–10 k. You don't need an exact calculation — what matters is the order-of-magnitude comparison.*

将 (b) 的结果与 (a) 对比。物理基底的"热力学承载能力"与它实际承载的"符号信息量"之间，大约差了几个数量级？这个差距意味着什么？假如人类的技术能将同一批文本用更少的原子来编码（比如用DNA分子替代纸张），这个差距会如何变化？

Compare your results from (b) and (a). Roughly how many orders of magnitude separate the "thermodynamic carrying capacity" of the physical substrate from the "symbolic information content" it actually carries? What does this gap imply? If technology could encode the same texts using far fewer atoms (e.g., DNA molecules instead of paper), how would this gap change?

**(c) 排列层：书架上的信息**

**(c) The Arrangement Layer: Information on the Shelves**

文本之外，信息还存在于书的物理排列中。假设图书馆采用中图分类法（或你自己熟悉的分类体系），将藏书排布在一个 10,000 个书架的三维网格中（每架 5 层，每层约 20 册）。

Beyond text, information also resides in the physical arrangement of the books. Suppose the library uses a classification system and arranges its collection across a three-dimensional grid of 10,000 bookshelves (5 shelves per rack, ~20 volumes per shelf).

(i) 每本书在三维空间中的位置（书架编号 + 层位 + 层内次序），大约需要多少比特来编码？

(i) How many bits are needed to encode each book's 3D position (shelf ID + tier + position within the tier)?

(ii) 但这仅仅是"位置的编码成本"。真正的问题是：**排列本身就是一种信息**——同一本书放在"哲学类第三排"和放在"新书推荐区入口处"，传递的含义完全不同。排列所编码的信息，能否用上面 (i) 的比特数来度量？为什么能或不能？

(ii) But that is merely the "coding cost of positions." The real question is: **the arrangement itself IS information** — a book placed on "Philosophy, Row 3" conveys a completely different meaning from the same book placed on "New Arrivals, Entrance Display." Can the information encoded by the arrangement be measured by the number of bits from (i)? Why or why not?

(iii) 一个对书架排列完全无知的读者走进图书馆。他每看到一个书架上的书脊标题组合，就减少了一部分"对这座图书馆里有什么"的不确定性。从这个角度看，**书架排列本身是否在传递信息？这个信息的熵与 (a) 中的文本熵之间是什么关系**——是叠加、包含、还是正交？

(iii) A reader who knows nothing about the shelving arrangement walks into the library. Every shelf of book spines she observes reduces her uncertainty about "what exists in this library." From this perspective, **does the shelving arrangement itself transmit information? What is the relationship between the entropy of this information and the textual entropy from (a)** — additive, nested, or orthogonal?

---

**(d) 建筑层：空间本身就是比特**

**(d) The Architecture Layer: Space Itself as Bits**

图书馆建筑本身——墙壁的位置、走廊的走向、楼梯的高度、窗户的朝向——都在编码信息。从正门进入后向左转是"自然科学区"而不是"文学区"，这个事实决定了每位读者的行走路径和发现顺序。

The library building itself — the positions of walls, the layout of corridors, the height of stairs, the orientation of windows — encodes information. That turning left from the main entrance leads to "Natural Sciences" rather than "Literature" determines every reader's walking path and discovery sequence.

(i) 假设图书馆建筑面积 20,000 m²，划分为 500 个功能空间（阅览室、书库、楼梯、卫生间等），每间有 4 种功能类型、3 种面积等级、2 种采光条件。仅从"空间拓扑"角度估算建筑布局的信息熵上限。

(i) Suppose the library covers 20,000 m² and is divided into 500 functional spaces (reading rooms, stacks, stairwells, restrooms, etc.), each with 4 possible function types, 3 size grades, and 2 lighting conditions. Estimate an upper bound on the information entropy of the building's layout from the "spatial topology" perspective alone.

(ii) （开放题）一栋建筑和一本关于这栋建筑的书，哪一个包含的信息更多？用你在 (a) 和 (d)(i) 中的估算，给出论证——然后反思这个论证的漏洞：**建筑和书"编码信息"的方式是否可通约？**

(ii) (Open) Which contains more information — a building, or a book about that building? Use your estimates from (a) and (d)(i) to make an argument — then reflect on its flaw: **are the ways a building and a book "encode information" commensurable at all?**

**(e) 缺失层与痕迹层：被排除的信息和被磨损的信息**

**(e) The Exclusion Layer and the Trace Layer: Information Excluded and Information Worn**

(i) **选书决策即信息。** 这座图书馆"选择收录了这 10^6 册书"而非另外 10^6 册书，这个选择本身是否携带着信息？假设世界上总共有 10^9 册不同的书，从其中恰好选出这 10^6 册——这个选择的信息熵是多少？这个信息被封存在图书馆的物理边界之内，但**它所编码的是"被排除的外部世界"**。这种"通过排除来编码"的信息，在计算科学中有什么类似的概念？

(i) **Selection is information.** The library "chose to include these 10^6 volumes" rather than a different 10^6. Does this choice itself carry information? Suppose there are 10^9 distinct books in the world, and exactly these 10^6 were selected — what is the information entropy of this selection? This information is locked within the library's physical boundaries, but **what it encodes is "the external world that was excluded."** What analogous concept in computing science encodes information "by exclusion"?

(ii) **磨损即书写。** 书架上某些书的书脊被磨得发白，某些书页的边角有折痕，某些页边写满了批注。一批读者在图书馆里留下的物理痕迹——如果我们能完整地、高精度地扫描所有这些痕迹，它们可被视为一段"读者行为的信息"。这段信息与 (a) 中的文本熵之间，是冗余的（只是文本的另一种物理表现）还是独立的（编码了文本不能编码的另一类知识）？

(ii) **Wear is writing.** Some books on the shelves have bleached spines from repeated handling, some have dog-eared pages, some margins are filled with annotations. The physical traces left by generations of readers — if we could scan all such traces with high precision — could be viewed as "information about reader behavior." Is this information redundant with the textual entropy from (a) (merely another physical manifestation of the text), or is it independent (encoding a different kind of knowledge that the text itself cannot encode)?

**(f) 综合题：图书馆的"总熵"**

**(f) Synthesis: The "Total Entropy" of a Library**

Review all your estimates and arguments from (a)–(e). Now answer:
