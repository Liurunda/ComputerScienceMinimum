# 练习 2 — 交互的历史弧线：你站在哪里

Exercise 2 — The Historical Arc of Interaction: Where Do You Stand

本章描绘了人机交互的历史弧线——"翻译负担"从人转移到机器。每一步削减了人必须"用机器的语言思考"的认知负载。

This chapter traced the historical arc of HCI — the "translation burden" shifting from human to machine. Each step reduces the cognitive load of a person having to "think in the machine's language."

---

**(1)** 你是否使用过命令行？如果使用过，描述一个你"必须用机器的思维方式来适应语法"的场景。这个负担是否让你排斥，还是让你感到"掌控感"？

Have you ever used a command line? If so, describe a scenario where you "had to adapt your thinking to the machine's syntax." Did this burden repel you, or give you a "sense of mastery"?

---

**(2)** 本章警告："完全流畅的交互会让人变成被动接收者"。你是同意还是反对？举一个你个人的例子（工具、平台、游戏）来论证。

This chapter warns: "completely fluid interaction turns people into passive recipients." Do you agree or disagree? Give a personal example (a tool, platform, or game) to argue your case.

---

**(3)** LLM 对话界面属于这条弧线上的哪一步？它是在继续"减少翻译负担"（你不需要学编程就能写代码），还是在创造新的负担（你必须学会如何 prompt）？

Where on this arc does the LLM conversational interface belong? Is it continuing the "reduction of translation burden" (you don't need to learn programming to write code), or is it creating a new burden (you must learn how to prompt)?

---

**(4)** 设计一个工具或应用的"交互黑洞"——它是否有一个让你感觉"自己失去了代理感"的环节？（参考："自动驾驶的乘客、"AI一键补全"的用户等，或一个你熟悉的例子）。用本章的四项原则诊断它缺了什么。

Designate an "interaction black hole" in a tool or app — is there a moment where you feel "you lost your sense of agency"? (References: a passenger in a self-driving car; a user of "AI one-click completion"; or any example you know well). Diagnose which of this chapter's four principles it violates.
