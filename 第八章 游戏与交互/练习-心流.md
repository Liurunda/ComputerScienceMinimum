# 练习 1 — 好玩的窄带：找到你的"心流"区间

Exercise 1 — The Narrow Band of Fun: Finding Your Flow Zone

本章声称"好玩存在于确定与随机之间的一个窄带里"——挑战恰好比技能高一点。

This chapter claims that "fun exists in a narrow band between determinism and randomness" — where challenge is just slightly above skill.

---

**(1)** 想一个你曾经沉迷的游戏（电子游戏、棋牌、运动均可）。它的哪个方面让你停不下来——是可预测的规则，还是不可预测的对手/随机事件？两者各占多少比例？

Think of a game you've been obsessed with (video game, board game, sport — any). What aspect made you unable to stop — predictable rules, or unpredictable opponents/random events? What is the proportion between the two?

---

**(2)** 你最终为什么停止了玩这个游戏？是太简单（技能超过挑战）还是太难（挑战远超技能）？或者是其他原因——比如耗尽了新内容（状态图被你遍历完了）？

Why did you eventually stop playing? Was it too easy (skill surpassed challenge), too hard (challenge far exceeded skill), or another reason — e.g., you exhausted all new content (the state graph was fully traversed)?

---

**(3)** 是否有过一个游戏（或工具），你刚接触时觉得"太难"、但通过一个"入门体验"逐渐上手？这个入门体验具体是什么设计？

Was there ever a game (or tool) you initially found "too hard," but gradually got into through an "onboarding experience"? What was the specific design of that onboarding experience?

---

**(4)** LLM 是一个"游戏"吗？当你和 LLM 对话时，你在"玩"什么？和它交互符合"挑战略高于技能的心流区间"吗？如果不是，它缺什么？

Is an LLM a "game"? When you converse with an LLM, what are you "playing"? Does interacting with it fit the "flow zone where challenge is slightly above skill"? If not, what is it missing?
