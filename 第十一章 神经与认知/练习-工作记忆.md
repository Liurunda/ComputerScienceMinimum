# 练习 — 人类工作记忆的容量：7±2 还是 4±1？

Exercise — The Capacity of Human Working Memory: 7±2 or 4±1?

你可以在脑中记住一个电话号码——不含区号的七位数字——从通讯录翻到拨号盘的那几秒。你是怎么做到的？那个"暂存"它的地方，心理学家称之为**工作记忆**（working memory）。它不是你"记住"一个东西一天以上的长时记忆（long-term memory），也不是你"刚才听到一个声音"但是两三秒后就消失的瞬时感觉记忆（sensory memory）。它是你的大脑在**维持并同时操作信息**的那一小块"临时的板子"——你的意识桌面。

You can hold a phone number — seven digits, minus the area code — in your head for the few seconds between your contacts list and the dial pad. How do you do that? The place where you "temporarily hold" it is what psychologists call **working memory**. It is not your long-term memory for things you remember over a day, nor your sensory memory — the fleeting trace of a sound that vanishes after two or three seconds. It is that small "temporary scratchpad" where your brain **maintains and simultaneously manipulates** information — the desktop of your consciousness.

这个桌面有多大？答案比你想象的有趣——因为它至今没有一个共识。

How big is this desktop? The answer is more interesting than you'd expect — because a consensus doesn't exist.

**(1)** 1956年，乔治·米勒（George Miller）发表了认知心理学史上被引用最多的论文之一——《神奇的数字 7±2》（The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two）。他发现：人们在一维刺激上做出绝对判断（absolute judgment）的能力——比如只凭听觉辨别不同音高的纯音——大约在 7 个类别之后就会开始混淆。与此同时，人们瞬时记住一串随机数字的长度也大约在这个范围。

In 1956, George Miller published one of the most cited papers in the history of cognitive psychology — "The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two." He found: people's ability for absolute judgment on unidimensional stimuli — e.g., identifying pure tones by pitch alone — begins to confuse beyond about 7 categories. Simultaneously, people's immediate memory span for a sequence of random digits also falls in roughly this range.

但米勒自己在这个论文的结尾说了一句常被忽略的话。他说——大致意思——"也许并没有那个神奇的数字7。7 只是一个'某个固定的瓶颈容量'的某种操作形式上的巧合。" 为什么米勒本人在提出这个数字的时候就播下了怀疑它的种子？他的核心洞见——在这篇论文的标题以外——到底是什么？

But Miller himself, at the end of this paper, said something often ignored. He said — roughly — "perhaps there is no magical number seven. Seven is merely a coincidence arising from some operational form of a fixed bottleneck capacity." Why did Miller himself sow the seed of doubt about this number even as he proposed it? Beyond the title, what was his core insight?

*提示：重读米勒论文的完整标题——"The Magical Number Seven, Plus or Minus Two: Some Limits on Our Capacity for Processing Information"。这个论文不是关于"记忆容量"的——它是关于**信息处理的容量**。而"处理信息"和"存储条目"是两件不同的事。Hint: re-read the full title of Miller's paper — it's about "limits on our capacity for processing information." It is not a paper about "memory capacity" — it is about **information-processing capacity**. And "processing information" and "storing items" are two different things.*

**(2)** 1974年，Alan Baddeley 和 Graham Hitch 提出了工作记忆的多成分模型（multi-component model），它不是一个单一的"7个槽位"，而是一个系统包含至少三个部分：**语音回路**（phonological loop，处理声音信息）、**视空间画板**（visuospatial sketchpad，处理图像和空间信息）、以及一个**中央执行器**（central executive，负责注意力的分配和协调）。

In 1974, Alan Baddeley and Graham Hitch proposed the multi-component model of working memory. It is not a unitary "7 slots," but a system containing at least three parts: the **phonological loop** (processing sound-based information), the **visuospatial sketchpad** (processing image and spatial information), and the **central executive** (responsible for allocating and coordinating attention).

请你做一个实验——先对自己做 (a) 再找一个朋友做 (b)：
(a) 念出以下数字序列，然后立即闭眼回忆——"3-7-1-9-4-2-6-8"。能完全正确回忆的最长序列是多长？
(b) 现在同时对朋友念数字（"3-7-1-9..."）和显示一个网格上的点位置（"...同时记住这个点在哪"）。此时朋友还能记住的数字个数前后有无变化？

Run an experiment — first on yourself (a), then find a friend for (b):
(a) Read aloud the following digit sequence, then immediately close your eyes and recall: "3-7-1-9-4-2-6-8." What is the longest sequence you can recall perfectly?
(b) Now, while verbally presenting digits to a friend ("3-7-1-9..."), simultaneously show them a dot location on a grid ("...and also remember where this dot is"). Does the number of digits they can recall change?

为什么双重任务（dual-task）干扰的是"记忆容量"而不是"注意力总量"——即，为什么数字任务只干扰语音型的记忆材料，而空间位置任务只干扰视觉型的记忆材料，两者并没有产生"一般的争抢"？这对"工作记忆是一个统一的7槽位池"这个朴素直觉来说，意味着什么？

Why does dual-task interference target "memory modality" rather than "total attentional capacity" — i.e., why does a digit task only interfere with verbal memory materials, while a spatial dot task only interferes with visual memory materials, rather than producing a "general competition"? What does this imply for the naive intuition that "working memory is a unified pool of 7 slots"?

**(3)** 2001年，Nelson Cowan 在《行为与脑科学》上发表了一篇猛烈攻击"7±2"的靶子论文。他重新分析了半个世纪的实验数据后得出结论：**工作记忆的"纯存储"核心容量——去除掉复述策略和组块化这些"作弊"手段后——更接近 4±1 个单元。** "7±2"是在"允许被试复述、允许他们暗中分组"的条件下观测到的表现——那不是存储的容量，那是**加工后的输出**。

In 2001, Nelson Cowan published a fierce attack on "7±2" as a target article in *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*. After reanalyzing half a century of experimental data, he concluded: the "pure storage" core capacity of working memory — stripped of rehearsal strategies and chunking "cheats" — is closer to **4 ± 1 units.** "7±2" is the performance observed under conditions where subjects are allowed to rehearse and covertly group items — that is not storage capacity, it is **processed output.**

这恰好是第二章和第三章合在一起的一个熟悉的主题。Cowan 说的"4±1"对应第二章什么概念？Miller 说的"7±2"对应第三章什么概念？如果"组块化（chunking）"是指——你用你已经知道的东西把多个独立的信息打包成一个——那么它是不是和第九章的"知识与压缩"在结构上同一回事？

This happens to be a familiar theme combining Chapter 2 and Chapter 3. Cowan's "4±1" corresponds to what concept in Chapter 2? Miller's "7±2" corresponds to what concept in Chapter 3? If "chunking" means — you use what you already know to pack multiple separate pieces of information into a single unit — is it structurally the same thing as Chapter 9's "knowledge and compression"?

*提示：第二章区分了"信息"（被消除的不确定性）和"数据"（承载信息的物理符号）。一个"组块"——比如"IBM"——对一个不知道 IBM 公司的人来说是三个字母（3 个数据），对一个知道的人来说是一个公司名（1 个信息条目）。组块化的是你的知识剪掉了"这三个字母的各种其它可能排列"。Hint: Chapter 2 distinguishes "information" (uncertainty eliminated) from "data" (the physical symbols carrying it). A "chunk" — like "IBM" — is three letters (3 data points) to someone who doesn't know the company, but one company name (1 information entry) to someone who does. Chunking works because your knowledge has pruned away "the other possible arrangements of these three letters."*

**(5)** 你可能会觉得——"这不就是一个关于人类记忆的心理学小问题吗？跟计算有什么关系？" 但工作记忆的容量限制，可能直接决定了我们的祖先能处理多复杂的社会结构。Robin Dunbar 发现人类自然社交网络的大小大约在 150 人（"Dunbar's number"）——而灵长类的新皮层比率（neocortex ratio）和群体的平均规模强相关。计算需要暂存中间结果；社交——"A 和 B 吵架了，B 是 C 的盟友，但 C 欠 D 一个人情..."——也需要在工作记忆里暂存**正在进行中的社会关系的引用**。如果 Cowan 的 4±1 是对的，那么你在单次瞬时注意中最多能同时追踪大约 4 个人之间的社交动态。一个推论：也许**语言的诞生、社会的分层、代码的发明——都是"工作记忆不够用"这件事的补偿机制。** 语言把想法卸到空气里给第二个人；文字把想法卸到石头上给后代；代码把"记住中间状态"这件事外包给变量名。这和本练习所在的第十一章的扩展心智假说形成了什么关联？

You might think — "Isn't this just a small psychology question about human memory? What does it have to do with computation?" But the capacity limit of working memory may have directly determined how complex a social structure our ancestors could handle. Robin Dunbar found that the natural size of human social networks is around 150 people ("Dunbar's number") — and the neocortex ratio of primates strongly correlates with average group size. Computation requires holding intermediate results; social life — "A and B had a fight, B is C's ally, but C owes D a favor..." — also requires holding **references to ongoing social relationships** in working memory. If Cowan's 4±1 is correct, you can track, at most, about 4 people's social dynamics simultaneously in a single moment of attention. A corollary: perhaps **the birth of language, the stratification of society, the invention of writing — are all compensation mechanisms for "working memory being not enough."** Language offloads thoughts into the air to a second person; writing offloads thoughts onto stone for future generations; code offloads "remembering intermediate states" onto variable names. What connection does this form with the Extended Mind Hypothesis in the same Chapter 11 that houses this exercise?

*提示：如果扩展心智假说是对的——你的笔记本、你的手机、你的朋友、你的变量名，都是你心智的物理延伸——那么"4±1"本身就不是一个"人的缺陷"，而是**物理世界中的生物基底的固定参数**。这个参数之所以重要，恰恰是因为外部心智结构（语言、文字、代码）就是围绕着它长出来的。Hint: if the Extended Mind Hypothesis is correct — your notebook, your phone, your friends, your variable names are all physical extensions of your mind — then "4±1" is not a "human defect," but a **fixed parameter of the biological substrate in the physical world.** This parameter matters precisely because the external mental structures (language, writing, code) grew around it.*
