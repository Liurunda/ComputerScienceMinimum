# 练习 — 麦卡洛克与皮茨：当神经元第一次被写成逻辑式

Exercise — McCulloch and Pitts: When a Neuron Was First Written as a Logical Formula

1943年，神经生理学家 Warren McCulloch 和一位18岁的自学天才 Walter Pitts 在《数学生物物理学通报》上发表了《神经活动中内在思想的逻辑演算》（A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity）。这篇论文第一次提出：**一个神经元可以被严格描述为一个逻辑单元——当它的兴奋性输入之和超过阈值时，它发放（fire）；否则它静息。从这个极其简单的基元出发，用网络连接，可以构造出任意的逻辑运算。** 这是人类历史上第一次在"心智"和"计算"之间建立的真正的等号——不是比喻，是数学模型。

In 1943, neurophysiologist Warren McCulloch and an 18-year-old self-taught prodigy Walter Pitts published "A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity" in the *Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics*. This paper proposed for the first time: **a neuron can be rigorously described as a logical unit — when the sum of its excitatory inputs exceeds a threshold, it fires; otherwise it remains quiescent. From this extraordinarily simple primitive, connected into networks, any arbitrary logical operation can be constructed.** This was the first moment in human history when a genuine equals sign — not a metaphor, but a mathematical model — was erected between "mind" and "computation."

**(1)** 麦卡洛克-皮茨神经元模型（MCP neuron）的规则极简：

The McCulloch-Pitts neuron model has a minimalist rule:

- 每个输入是兴奋性的（权重 +1）或抑制性的（权重足够负，使得只要有任何一个抑制性输入激活，神经元就一定不发放）
- 当兴奋性输入的被激活个数 ≥ 阈值 θ 且没有抑制性输入被激活时，神经元发放（输出 1）
- Each input is either excitatory (weight +1) or inhibitory (weight sufficiently negative such that if any single inhibitory input is active, the neuron is guaranteed not to fire)
- When the number of active excitatory inputs ≥ threshold θ AND no inhibitory input is active, the neuron fires (outputs 1)

请用纸笔画出用 MCP 神经元实现一个**逻辑与（AND）**门的网络：两个输入 A 和 B，一个输出。标注每个连接的兴奋/抑制属性以及神经元的阈值。

Using pen and paper, draw a network of MCP neurons implementing a logical **AND** gate: two inputs A and B, one output. Label each connection's excitatory/inhibitory property and the neuron's threshold.

*提示：你需要 θ = 2。AND 只有在 A 和 B 都激活时才输出 1。Hint: you need θ = 2. AND outputs 1 only when both A and B are active.*

**(2)** 麦卡洛克和皮茨在 1943 年证明了一个惊人的定理：**任何"图灵可计算的函数"——即任何可以用算法描述的过程——都可以由 MCP 神经元组成的网络实现。** 用今天的语言说：MCP 网络是图灵完备的。这个结论对第十七章"计算理论"来说很重要，但本章关心的是它对笛卡尔的冲击。如果神经元网络可以计算任何函数，**笛卡尔的"我思故我在"还剩下什么？为什么说这篇论文第一次让"机器中的幽灵"不再是必需品？**

McCulloch and Pitts proved a stunning theorem in 1943: **any "Turing-computable function" — i.e., any process describable by an algorithm — can be realized by a network of MCP neurons.** In today's language: MCP networks are Turing-complete. This conclusion is important for Chapter 17's "Theory of Computation," but what matters for this chapter is its impact on Descartes. If a network of neurons can compute any function, **what remains of Descartes' "I think therefore I am"? Why did this paper make the "ghost in the machine" unnecessary for the first time?**

*提示：如果神经元的物理活动已经可以执行任何逻辑运算——那么"思维"就不需要一个独立于身体的、非物理的灵魂来完成"思考"这件事。Cogito 不需要 ghost。Hint: if the physical activity of neurons can already carry out any logical operation — then "thinking" does not require an immaterial soul independent of the body to perform the act of "thinking." The cogito doesn't need a ghost.*

**(3)** Walter Pitts 本人的故事同样重要——它的意义，不亚于论文本身。Pitts 生于 1923 年，在底特律一个贫困的家庭长大，十几岁时因为被霸凌而自学希腊语、拉丁语、逻辑学和数学——全部躲在图书馆里。15 岁时，他读了罗素的《数学原理》后，写了一封信给罗素指出了书中的一处错误。罗素回信邀请他来剑桥学习，但 Pitts 没有去——他那时只是个孩子。18 岁时，他步行到了芝加哥，没有注册，只是走进 Rudolf Carnap 的课堂听课。后来他遇到了McCulloch，他们开始合作。**在没有大学学位、没有实验室、没有任何身份的情况下，他写下了这篇改变了历史的论文。**

Pitts was born in 1923 and grew up in a poor family in Detroit. Bullied as a teenager, he taught himself Greek, Latin, logic, and mathematics — all while hiding in the library. At age 15, after reading Russell's *Principia Mathematica*, he wrote Russell a letter pointing out an error in the book. Russell replied inviting him to study at Cambridge, but Pitts didn't go — he was just a child. At 18, he walked to Chicago and simply walked into Rudolf Carnap's lectures without enrolling. He later met McCulloch, and they began working together. **Without a university degree, without a laboratory, without any institutional identity, he wrote the paper that changed history.**

这一章本身就在论证：**心智的物理位置不在任何单一的脑区——甚至不在脑内。** Pitt 的故事是否是对"扩展心智假说"的另一个维度上的支持——也即，不是**从生物学的方向论证**心智不必要"在一个中心执行"，而且是**从社会/教育制度的角度证明**：一个足以理解心智本质的心智，不一定对应任何学历证明的"中心"？请随意讨论。

This chapter itself argues: **the physical locus of mind is not in any single brain region — and not even inside the brain.** Does Pitts' story support the Extended Mind Hypothesis from a different angle — namely, not **proving from the biological direction** that mind doesn't need "a central executor," but **proving from the social/educational-institutional direction** that a mind capable of understanding the nature of mind does not necessarily correspond to any credential-certified "center"? Discuss freely.

**(4)** 麦卡洛克-皮茨模型是生物神经元的一个**有损压缩**——真实神经元有连续的非线性响应、树突的复杂几何结构、突触强度的可变权重、神经递质和调质的化学多样性。正如第十章的"课程评分把一个高维的能力向量映射到了一维分数"，麦卡洛克和皮茨把生物学的复杂性映射到了一个只有{0, 1}二值状态的逻辑单元。但是——正是因为这个**压缩**，他们才发现了一个其它人看不到的东西。这个**"通过有损压缩来发现结构"**的原则，在本书哪些章节中出现过？

The McCulloch-Pitts model is a **lossy compression** of a biological neuron — real neurons have continuous nonlinear responses, complex dendritic geometries, variable synaptic weights, and chemical diversity of neurotransmitters and neuromodulators. Just as Chapter 10's "course grading maps a high-dimensional ability vector onto a one-dimensional score," McCulloch and Pitts mapped biological complexity onto a logical unit with only {0, 1} binary states. Yet — precisely because of this **compression**, they discovered something no one else could see. In which other chapters of this book does the principle of **"discovering structure through lossy compression"** appear?

**(5)** 最后回到第十一章的两条主线：笛卡尔认为思维是一个私人的、内在的过程。维特根斯坦说——不，你必须用公共语言。如果把这两条线放到麦卡洛克-皮茨模型身上——**麦卡洛克-皮茨神经元是在模拟笛卡尔的"我在里面想"，还是在模拟维特根斯坦的"想发生在公开可观察的逻辑关系中"？** 从 MCP 模型开始，"理解大脑"这件事，就变成了"理解神经元网络中的信号传播规则"——这是一个可以用第三人称公开观察、分析、复现的对象。笛卡尔的"第一人称私密性"——那个躲在内省后面的"我"——在这篇论文之后，是否已经可以置之不问？

Finally, return to the two threads of Chapter 11: Descartes believed thinking is a private, internal process. Wittgenstein said — no, you must use public language. If we place these two threads before the McCulloch-Pitts model — **is the McCulloch-Pitts neuron modeling Descartes' "I think inside," or is it modeling Wittgenstein's "thinking happens in publicly observable logical relations"?** Starting from the MCP model, "understanding the brain" became "understanding the rules of signal propagation in a neural network" — an object that can be publicly observed, analyzed, and reproduced in the third person. Descartes' "first-person privacy" — that "I" hiding behind introspection — after this paper, can it be set aside as a question no longer needing to be asked?

*提示：如果"思考"可以被第三方用逻辑式完整描述——那么笛卡尔从"我思"推导出的"我在（作为非物质的思考实体）"还能成立吗？Hint: if "thinking" can be fully described by a third party in logical formulas — can Descartes' inference from "I think" to "I exist (as an immaterial thinking substance)" still hold?*
