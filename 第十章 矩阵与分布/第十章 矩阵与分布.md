# 第十章 矩阵与分布

Chapter 10: Matrices and Distributions

大学课程评分的本质困难在于，学生能力和水平首先是一个高维向量或矩阵。如果用一些真正准确科学的方式衡量这个向量的模长或这个矩阵的特征值，得出来的结果在人群中应符合幂律分布（power-law distribution）。但课程评分，不得不将学生水平映射到一维数轴上的正态分布。

The fundamental difficulty of course grading is this: a student's ability is, first and foremost, a high-dimensional vector or matrix. If one were to measure this vector's magnitude or the eigenvalues of this matrix with truly accurate, scientific methods, the results across a population would follow a power-law distribution. Yet course grading is forced to map student ability onto a one-dimensional number line following a normal distribution.

本章的目的，就是简要解释这句话中的每一个概念，以及它们为什么重要。

The purpose of this chapter is to briefly explain each concept in this sentence, and why they matter.

---

## 向量

## Vectors

一个学生的能力不是一个数字。一个学生在数学推理、空间直觉、记忆力、持久力、创造力、口头表达——这些维度上的能力各自不同。如果我们选出 n 个可测量的维度，这个学生的能力就是一个 n 维向量：v = (v₁, v₂, ..., vₙ)。

A student's ability is not a single number. A student's ability in mathematical reasoning, spatial intuition, memory, persistence, creativity, verbal expression — these dimensions each differ. If we select n measurable dimensions, this student's ability is an n-dimensional vector: v = (v₁, v₂, ..., vₙ).

两个学生成绩相同（都在第 1 维度上得了 85 分）——但一个靠记忆力和持久力（第 3、4 维极高），另一个靠创造力和直觉（第 5、6 维极高）。把它们压缩成同一个数字，就抹去了这个差异。这是第三章"有损压缩"在人的维度上最普遍也最暴力的一种应用。

Two students get the same grade (85 on dimension 1) — but one achieved it through memory and persistence (extremely high on dimensions 3 and 4), while the other through creativity and intuition (extremely high on dimensions 5 and 6). Compressing them into the same number erases this difference. This is one of the most universal and violent applications of Chapter 3's "lossy compression" — applied to the dimension of the human.

---

## 矩阵

## Matrices

全班 30 个学生，每个人一个 n 维能力向量。将这 30 个向量按行排列，得到一个 30 × n 的矩阵。

A class of 30 students, each with an n-dimensional ability vector. Arrange these 30 vectors as rows — you get a 30 × n matrix.

矩阵不是"一堆数字的表格"。矩阵是**线性变换**——它将一个向量空间映射到另一个向量空间。这个班的"能力矩阵"，是将每个学生的真实能力向量（n 维）映射到分数向量（k 维，通常 k = 1）的变换的——**参数**。

A matrix is not "a table of numbers." A matrix is a **linear transformation** — it maps one vector space to another. This class's "ability matrix" is the — **parameter** — of the transformation mapping each student's true ability vector (n dimensions) to a grade vector (k dimensions, usually k = 1).

---

## 特征值与模长

## Eigenvalues and Magnitude

一个矩阵作用于某些特殊向量时，其结果恰好等于该向量乘以一个标量。这些向量就是**特征向量**，这些标量就是**特征值**。

When a matrix acts on certain special vectors, the result is exactly that same vector multiplied by a scalar. These vectors are **eigenvectors**; these scalars are **eigenvalues**.

在学生的能力矩阵中，最大的特征值对应一个方向——在能力空间中"这个方向"上学生之间的差异被最大程度地保留和放大。这个方向可能是"数学推理"的某个加权组合，也可能是"创造力+持久力"的某种配比。特征向量告诉你在能力空间中**哪个方向上的差异最大**。传统的课程评分——一场 3 小时的笔试——只测量了这个巨大矩阵中某一个特殊方向（通常数学+记忆）上的投影，然后把那个投影称为"分数"。

In a student ability matrix, the largest eigenvalue corresponds to a direction — "this direction" in ability space along which differences among students are maximally preserved and amplified. That direction might be some weighted combination of "mathematical reasoning," or some blend of "creativity + persistence." The eigenvector tells you **which direction in ability space has the greatest variance.** Traditional course grading — a 3-hour written exam — measures only the projection along one particular direction in this enormous matrix (typically mathematics + memory), and then labels that projection "the grade."

模长是对一个向量的——"这个人在能力空间中的总体强度"的一个标量估计。但一个人的能力不可能是一个标量。**模长不是"真实能力"，它是量化这件事时你得到的唯一一个一致的——但不完整的——结果。**

Magnitude is what is applied to a vector — a scalar estimate of "this person's overall strength in ability space." But a person's ability cannot be a scalar. **Magnitude is not "true ability"; it is the one consistent — but incomplete — result you get when you force quantification onto this thing.**

---

## 幂律

## Power Laws

现在假设——仅作为思想实验——我们有了一种真正科学的方式，可以测量每个学生的能力向量的模长（或该班成绩矩阵的最大特征值）。我们测量了一万名学生的模长，然后按数值从高到低排列。这些数值会如何分布？

Now suppose — as a thought experiment — we had a truly scientific way to measure the magnitude of each student's ability vector (or the largest eigenvalue of the class grade matrix). We measure the magnitudes of ten thousand students and sort them from high to low. How would these values be distributed?

**它们不服从正态分布。没有人会服从。** 人类在几乎所有"真实的、未被标准化压缩过的"能力指标上的分布都是幂律——极少数人的测量值可以是普通人的 10 倍、100 倍、甚至 1000 倍。第 2 名的值是第 1 名的一半；第 10 名的值是第 1 名的十分之一；第 1000 名的值几乎靠近零。这和第六章"尺度无关网络"（或者说，对数尺度网络）一节的幂律度分布，属于同一个数学家族。

**They do not follow a normal distribution. Nothing would.** The distribution of humans on almost any "real, unstandardized, uncompressed" ability metric follows a power law — an extremely small number of people score 10×, 100×, or even 1000× higher than the average. The #2 value is half of #1; the #10 value is a tenth of #1; the #1000 value hugs near zero. This belongs to the same mathematical family as the power-law degree distribution discussed in Chapter 6's "scale-free networks" (or rather, log-scale networks) section.

幂律不仅在人的能力上出现。它出现在城市人口、论文引用数、单词出现频率、地震震级、战争中阵亡人数的分布中。它在数学上是"当一个系统中存在富者愈富的正反馈机制"的自然结果——已经在这条维度上很强的人，由于这种优势，会在同样的时间内获得比其他人更多的增益。

Power laws appear not only in human ability. They appear in the distribution of city populations, paper citations, word frequencies, earthquake magnitudes, and war casualties. Mathematically, they are the natural result of "a system containing positive-feedback loops where the rich get richer" — a person already strong along this dimension, due to this very advantage, gains more than others in the same amount of time.

---

## 正态分布

## The Normal Distribution

但课程评分的输出不是幂律——是正态分布。或至少是"该班的成绩被期望是一个正态分布"。为什么？

But the output of course grading is not a power law — it is a normal distribution. Or at least "the class's grades are expected to follow a normal distribution." Why?

**因为你强制降维了。** 你把每一个学生的高维能力向量沿着某一条轴（"考试分数"）投影——一个 n 维的东西被压缩到 1 维。而中心极限定理说：许多独立的、来自任意分布的随机变量之和，趋向于正态分布。一个学生一场考试的得分，是在 n 个不同维度上的能力——每一维都受到当天的状态、考题的措辞、题目命中的知识面等因素的独立扰动——的加权求和。这个求和的结果趋近于正态分布，**不是因为学生的能力是正态分布的，而是因为每一次跨维度的求和都必然产生一个正态分布。**

**Because you forced dimensionality reduction.** You projected each student's high-dimensional ability vector along a single axis ("exam score") — an n-dimensional thing compressed into 1 dimension. And the Central Limit Theorem says: the sum of many independent random variables from arbitrary distributions tends toward a normal distribution. A student's score on one exam is a weighted sum of abilities along n different dimensions — each dimension independently perturbed by the state of the day, the phrasing of the question, the knowledge area hit by the topics. The result of this summation is approximately normally distributed, **not because students' abilities are normally distributed, but because every cross-dimensional summation necessarily produces a normal distribution.**

你用求和（考试）把幂律压成了正态。

You used summation (the exam) to flatten a power law into a normal curve.

---

## 为什么这重要

## Why This Matters

这不是"数学的一个有趣侧面"。这句话意味着：**那个数字——"85 分"——不是关于你的。它是关于你和你被测量那天所有随机因素的一次加权求和在你身上的表现。**

This is not "an interesting side note in mathematics." It means: **that number — "85 points" — is not about you. It is about the performance on your body of one weighted sum of you and all the random factors from the day you were measured.**

你没办法从那个数字里反解出你的能力向量。降维是不可逆的。这是有损压缩——而且是压缩得最狠的那种——n 个维度变成一列——不能"解压"回原来的自己。

You cannot recover your ability vector from that number. Dimensionality reduction is irreversible. This is lossy compression — the most brutal kind — n dimensions collapsed into a single column — impossible to "decompress" back into the original self.

---

你未来一生都会被人投射到各种"一维数轴"上——GPA，KPI，信用分，影响力指数。**这些数字没有一个是你。** 它们是在某一个特定的矩阵运算中，你的能力向量在那条特定轴上的投影。

For the rest of your life, you will be projected onto various "one-dimensional number lines" — GPA, KPI, credit score, influence index. **None of these numbers is you.** They are the projection of your ability vector onto one specific axis, under one specific matrix operation.

你的能力，是一整个高维空间。你不可能用一个数字替换它。

Your ability is an entire high-dimensional space. You cannot replace it with a single number.
