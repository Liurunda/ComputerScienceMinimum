# 练习 — 随机矩阵理论入门：当"随机"有了形状

Exercise — Introduction to Random Matrix Theory: When "Randomness" Takes Shape

第十章讲了矩阵的特征值——在学生的能力矩阵中，最大特征值对应"能力空间中差异最大的方向"。但如果我们面对的矩阵——它的每一个元素都是随机抽取的——它的特征值会如何分布？这个问题听起来像是纯数学的好奇心，但随机矩阵理论（Random Matrix Theory, RMT）在过去七十年里反复出现在核物理、数论、无线通信、机器学习、金融风险分析中。本练习带你走进这个领域的门槛。

Chapter 10 discussed eigenvalues of matrices — in a student ability matrix, the largest eigenvalue corresponds to "the direction of greatest variance in ability space." But what if the matrix we face — every element of it drawn at random — how would its eigenvalues be distributed? This question sounds like pure mathematical curiosity, yet Random Matrix Theory (RMT) has repeatedly appeared over the past seventy years in nuclear physics, number theory, wireless communications, machine learning, and financial risk analysis. This exercise walks you through the doorstep of this field.

---

**(1)** 设 A 是一个 n × n 的实对称矩阵，其上三角部分的每个元素独立地从均值为 0、方差为 1 的正态分布 N(0,1) 中抽取，下三角由对称性补全。这个矩阵有多少个独立的随机变量？它的特征值一定是实数吗？为什么？

Let A be an n × n real symmetric matrix whose upper-triangular entries are each independently drawn from the normal distribution N(0,1) with mean 0 and variance 1; the lower triangle is filled by symmetry. How many independent random variables does this matrix have? Are its eigenvalues guaranteed to be real? Why?

*提示：实对称矩阵的对角线元素也是独立抽取的。Hint: the diagonal entries of a real symmetric matrix are also drawn independently.*

---

**(2)** Wigner 半圆律（Wigner's Semicircle Law）是随机矩阵理论的第一块基石：当 n 很大时，上述随机矩阵的特征值的经验分布趋近于一个半圆形——

Wigner's Semicircle Law is the first cornerstone of RMT: when n is large, the empirical distribution of eigenvalues of the above random matrix approaches a semicircle —

$$\rho(x) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \sqrt{4 - x^2}, \quad x \in [-2, 2]$$

用 Python（或你熟悉的任何语言）生成一个 n=1000 的上述随机矩阵，用数值方法计算它的所有特征值，画出直方图，并在同一张图上叠加半圆律的曲线。你看到了什么？

Using Python (or any language you're comfortable with), generate one such random matrix with n=1000, compute all its eigenvalues numerically, plot their histogram, and overlay the semicircle curve on the same plot. What do you see?

*提示：numpy.random.randn 生成 N(0,1) 样本，numpy.linalg.eigvalsh 计算实对称矩阵的特征值。Hint: numpy.random.randn generates N(0,1) samples; numpy.linalg.eigvalsh computes eigenvalues of real symmetric matrices.*

---

**(3)** 第十章说课程评分把"幂律压成了正态"——中心极限定理在其中起了关键作用。半圆律是否也是某种"中心极限定理"——只不过对象从"独立随机变量之和"变成了"随机矩阵的特征值"？请从直觉上解释：为什么大量独立随机变量的叠加会必然产生某种确定的形状（无论这个形状是正态曲线还是半圆）？

Chapter 10 said course grading "flattened a power law into a normal curve" — the Central Limit Theorem played a key role. Is the semicircle law also a kind of "central limit theorem" — except the object has shifted from "sum of independent random variables" to "eigenvalues of a random matrix"? Explain intuitively: why does the superposition of many independent random variables inevitably produce some definite shape (whether that shape is a normal curve or a semicircle)?

*提示：想想第六章"熵"与"最概然分布"的关系——当系统的微观状态数量极大时，哪些宏观状态最可能出现？Hint: think about the relationship between "entropy" and "most probable distribution" from Chapter 6 — when the number of microscopic configurations is enormous, which macroscopic states are most likely to appear?*

---

**(4)** 普适性（Universality）是随机矩阵理论最惊人的发现之一：你把上三角元素的分布从 N(0,1) 换成均匀分布 U(-1,1)，甚至换成"以 1/2 概率取 +1、以 1/2 概率取 -1" 的离散分布——只要均值为 0、方差有限——当 n→∞ 时特征值的分布仍然趋近于同一个半圆。换句话说，**微观的随机性在宏观上被"洗掉"了**。这和第十章哪一段落的核心思想形成了精确的平行关系？

Universality is one of the most striking discoveries of RMT: replace the distribution of upper-triangular entries from N(0,1) to the uniform distribution U(-1,1), or even to a discrete distribution "take +1 with probability 1/2 and -1 with probability 1/2" — as long as the mean is 0 and the variance is finite — the eigenvalue distribution as n→∞ still approaches the same semicircle. In other words, **microscopic randomness is "washed out" at the macroscopic level.** Which passage from Chapter 10 forms a precise parallel to this idea?

*提示：重读第十章"正态分布"一节中关于中心极限定理的论述。Hint: re-read the discussion of the Central Limit Theorem in Chapter 10's "Normal Distribution" section.*

---

**(5)** 如果随机矩阵理论告诉我们"一个足够大的随机矩阵，其特征值分布是可以精确预测的"，那么回到第十章的学生能力矩阵：假如你手上真的有一个 10000 × 100 的学生能力矩阵，你怎么判断它的特征值分布是"有意义的信号"（反映了学生能力的真实结构）还是"纯噪声"（和随机矩阵没有显著差异）？

If RMT tells us that "for a sufficiently large random matrix, its eigenvalue distribution is precisely predictable," then returning to Chapter 10's student ability matrix: suppose you actually had a 10000 × 100 student ability matrix. How would you determine whether its eigenvalue distribution is "meaningful signal" (reflecting real structure in student ability) versus "pure noise" (not significantly different from a random matrix)?

*提示：这并非一个纯假想问题——今天在金融、基因组学、神经科学中，研究者用随机矩阵理论作为"零假设"来检出真实信号。最大的那几个特征值如果明显超出半圆律的上界（[−2, 2]），它们代表结构；其余落在半圆内的特征值，很可能是噪声。Hint: this is not a purely hypothetical question — today in finance, genomics, and neuroscience, researchers use RMT as the "null hypothesis" to detect real signal. The largest few eigenvalues, if they clearly exceed the semicircle's upper bound ([−2, 2]), represent structure; the remaining eigenvalues within the semicircle are likely noise.*
