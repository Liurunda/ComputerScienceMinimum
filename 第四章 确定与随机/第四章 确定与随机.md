# 确定与随机

Determinism and Randomness

牛顿力学描绘了一个钟表宇宙：给定每一粒子的位置和速度，宇宙的整个未来——从行星轨道到茶杯上的裂纹——都已被唯一确定。拉普拉斯将这个思想推至极致：如果一个智灵知晓此刻所有粒子的状态和所有物理定律，那么"未来和过去将同样地呈现在它的眼前"。

Newtonian mechanics painted a clockwork universe: given the position and velocity of every particle, the entire future of the universe — from planetary orbits to the crack in a teacup — is uniquely determined. Laplace pushed this idea to its limit: if an intellect knew the state of all particles at this moment and all physical laws, then "the future and the past would be equally present before its eyes."

---

然而，第二章的玻尔兹曼已经告诉我们：这个钟表宇宙的图景，在最根本的层面上，和热力学第二定律无法调和。一杯热水放在房间里，分子层面的牛顿方程在时间反演下完全对称——把 t 换成 -t，每个分子的轨道仍然合法。但热水永远不会自动变热。为什么？因为"所有分子能量均匀分布"的热平衡状态，在状态空间中占据了压倒性的概率。**确定性方程无法解释不可逆性；不可逆性需要用概率——用对微观状态的无知——来度量。**

Yet Boltzmann in Chapter 2 already told us: this clockwork universe picture, at the most fundamental level, is irreconcilable with the Second Law of Thermodynamics. A cup of hot water placed in a room — at the molecular level, Newton's equations are perfectly symmetric under time reversal. Replace t with -t, and every molecular trajectory is still lawful. Yet the hot water never spontaneously gets hotter. Why? Because thermal equilibrium — where molecular energies are evenly spread — occupies the overwhelming probability in state space. **Deterministic equations cannot explain irreversibility; irreversibility must be measured with probability — with ignorance of the microstates.**

---

量子力学将这个问题推得更深。薛定谔方程本身是确定性的——波函数按照一个确定的微分方程演化。但"测量"这一瞬间发生了什么？波函数坍缩是确定性的还是随机的？哥本哈根诠释说它是随机的；多世界诠释说它只是看起来随机——因为观察者本人也纠缠进了叠加态。这个争论至今没有定论。但计算科学不必等待物理学达成共识：无论物理基底到底是确定的还是随机的，**计算系统的设计者必须在同一个系统中同时驾驭确定性和随机性。**

Quantum mechanics pushes this question even deeper. The Schrödinger equation itself is deterministic — the wavefunction evolves according to a deterministic differential equation. But what happens at the moment of "measurement"? Is wavefunction collapse deterministic or random? The Copenhagen interpretation says it is random; the Many-Worlds interpretation says it only appears random — because the observer themselves becomes entangled in the superposition. This debate remains unsettled. But computing science need not wait for physics to reach consensus: whether the physical substrate is ultimately deterministic or random, **the designer of computing systems must master both determinism and randomness within the same system.**

---

## 随机性作为计算资源

## Randomness as a Computational Resource

香农的信息熵 H = -Σ p log p 是建立在概率分布之上的。如果一个信源是彻底确定的——每个符号都以概率 1 出现——那么它的熵为零，不携带任何信息。**没有随机性，就没有"信息量"这个概念。** 随机性在这里不是噪音，它是信息的水源。

Shannon's information entropy H = -Σ p log p is built upon probability distributions. If a source is completely deterministic — every symbol appears with probability 1 — its entropy is zero, and it carries no information. **Without randomness, there is no concept of "information quantity."** Randomness here is not noise; it is the wellspring of information.

---

但随机性的价值远不止定义信息。它可以被**主动注入**到算法中，作为一种计算资源来使用：

But the value of randomness goes far beyond defining information. It can be **actively injected** into algorithms and used as a computational resource:

快速排序的最坏情况——每次 pivot 恰好选到当前子数组的最小值——时间复杂度是 O(n²)。但当你**随机选择 pivot**，最坏情况发生的概率变得极小。随机化快排不是在算法本身上超越了确定性的堆排序（两者都是 O(n log n) 期望时间），但它用极其简单的代码达到了同样的效率。**随机性在这里被用来打破"最坏情况输入"的魔咒——它在输入分布上撒了一层概率的雾，让最坏情况变得几乎不可触及。**

Quicksort's worst case — each pivot happens to be the minimum of the current subarray — is O(n²). But when you **choose the pivot randomly**, the probability of hitting the worst case becomes vanishingly small. Randomized quicksort does not surpass deterministic heapsort algorithmically (both are O(n log n) expected), but it achieves the same efficiency with far simpler code. **Randomness is used here to break the curse of "worst-case input" — it spreads a probabilistic fog over the input distribution, making the worst case nearly unreachable.**

---

蒙特卡洛方法将这一策略推向了更广阔的领域。计算一个高维积分——比如 100 维空间中一个复杂形状的体积——确定性数值积分（在网格上采样）的代价随维度指数增长（"维度灾难"）。而蒙特卡洛方法随机撒点，误差按 1/√N 收敛——**与维度无关**。在高维空间，随机性比确定性更高效。这不是工程上的妥协，这是数学上的事实。

Monte Carlo methods push this strategy into even broader territory. Computing a high-dimensional integral — say, the volume of a complex shape in 100-dimensional space — deterministic numerical integration (sampling on a grid) costs exponentially with dimension (the "curse of dimensionality"). Monte Carlo methods scatter points randomly, and the error converges at 1/√N — **independent of dimension**. In high-dimensional spaces, randomness is more efficient than determinism. This is not an engineering compromise; it is a mathematical fact.

---

密码学将随机性推到了安全性的根基位置。一个加密密钥如果可以被对手猜到，加密算法再优雅也没有用。现代密码学建立在对手"计算上无法区分"伪随机和真随机的假设之上。一个系统的熵池耗尽——即系统中的随机性被用光——导致的漏洞，比任何密码算法本身的缺陷都更致命。

Cryptography places randomness at the very foundation of security. If an encryption key can be guessed by an adversary, the elegance of the encryption algorithm is irrelevant. Modern cryptography rests on the assumption that adversaries are "computationally unable to distinguish" pseudo-randomness from true randomness. A vulnerability caused by a system's entropy pool running dry — the system running out of randomness — is more fatal than any flaw in the cryptographic algorithm itself.

---

从这些例子中可以提炼出一条统一的原则：**随机性是一种可以在"正确性的确定程度"和"计算成本"之间做 tradeoff 的杠杆。** 你接受了一点点"可能出错"的概率，换来的是大幅降低的时间复杂度、空间复杂度或代码复杂度。这和第二章兰道尔界限的逻辑同构：计算在物理世界不是免费的，在概率世界也不是免费的——你用"确定性"作为货币，购买了"效率"。

From these examples, a unifying principle emerges: **randomness is a lever for trading off between "degree of certainty in correctness" and "computational cost."** You accept a tiny probability of being wrong, and in exchange, you get drastically reduced time complexity, space complexity, or code complexity. This is structurally isomorphic to the logic of Landauer's bound in Chapter 2: computation is not free in the physical world, and it is not free in the probabilistic world either — you spend "determinism" as currency to purchase "efficiency."

## 伪随机：确定性机器如何制造随机

## Pseudo-randomness: How a Deterministic Machine Manufactures Randomness

冯·诺依曼说过："任何人如果考虑用算术方法来产生随机数字，那他肯定是有罪的。"但我们就这么干了。

Von Neumann famously said: "Any one who considers arithmetical methods of producing random digits is, of course, in a state of sin." And yet we did it anyway.

---

一个伪随机数生成器（PRNG）是一个确定性的有限状态机：它从前一个状态 s_n 通过一个确定性的转移函数 f 计算出下一个状态 s_{n+1} = f(s_n)，然后从状态中提取出"看起来随机"的输出。整个序列完全由初始种子（seed）决定——**种子决定了所有**。

A pseudo-random number generator (PRNG) is a deterministic finite state machine: it computes the next state s_{n+1} = f(s_n) from the previous state through a deterministic transition function f, then extracts a "random-looking" output from the state. The entire sequence is completely determined by the initial seed — **the seed determines everything.**

---

但这引出了一个深刻的哲学问题：如果一段序列在所有的统计测试下都无法与真随机区分，那么它和真随机的区别在哪里？答案在"知道种子的人"和"不知道种子的人"之间划出了一条线。对于不知道种子的人，这个序列就是随机的——它的不可预测性不来自物理世界的量子噪声，而来自**计算上的不可行性**：从输出反推种子（或预测下一个输出）所需要的计算量超过了攻击者的算力上限。

This raises a deep philosophical question: if a sequence is statistically indistinguishable from true randomness under all tests, then where exactly does it differ from true randomness? The answer draws a line between "those who know the seed" and "those who don't." For someone who doesn't know the seed, the sequence is random — its unpredictability arises not from quantum noise in the physical world, but from **computational infeasibility**: the computation required to reverse-engineer the seed from the output (or predict the next output) exceeds the adversary's computational upper bound.

---

这和第三章的"黑话"现象形成了精确的对偶：黑话对圈外人不可解，对圈内人一目了然——因为圈内人拥有"解压字典"。伪随机序列对不知道种子的人不可预测，对知道种子的人**完全确定**——因为种子就是那个"解码密钥"。**"随机性"不是序列的客观属性，而是观察者与序列之间的关系。** 一个序列是否是随机的，取决于你站在种子的哪一边。

This forms a precise duality with the "jargon" phenomenon from Chapter 3: jargon is incomprehensible to outsiders but crystal-clear to insiders — because insiders possess the "decompression dictionary." A pseudo-random sequence is unpredictable to those without the seed yet **completely deterministic** to those with it — because the seed is the "decoding key." **"Randomness" is not an objective property of a sequence, but a relationship between the observer and the sequence.** Whether a sequence is random depends on which side of the seed you stand on.

## LLM 中的随机性：温度与采样

## Randomness in LLMs: Temperature and Sampling

第三章讨论了受控自然语言（CNL）如何约束 LLM 的输出。但 LLM 的工作方式本身就将"确定与随机"的张力直接嵌入到了推理的每一步中。

Chapter 3 discussed how controlled natural language (CNL) constrains LLM outputs. But the way LLMs work directly embeds the tension between determinism and randomness into every step of inference.

---

LLM 的推理——next token prediction——的每一步输出不是一个确定的词，而是一个**概率分布**覆盖在整个词汇表上。模型说："下一个词，'猫'的概率是 0.3，'狗'是 0.2，'桌子'是 0.001。"然后你必须选择一个词。怎么选？

At each step of LLM inference — next token prediction — the output is not a single determined word, but a **probability distribution** over the entire vocabulary. The model says: "The next word: 'cat' with probability 0.3, 'dog' with 0.2, 'table' with 0.001." And then you must choose a word. How?

---

温度参数 T 控制了这个选择过程中的**随机性剂量**。当 T → 0（贪婪解码），模型总是选择概率最高的那个词——输出变成完全确定性的。同一段 prompt，每次生成的文本完全相同。当 T = 1，模型按原始概率分布采样——高概率的词更可能被选中，但低概率的词偶尔也能冒出来。当 T > 1，概率分布被"抹平"——罕见词的出现频率被人为提高，输出变得更加不可预测。

The temperature parameter T controls the **dosage of randomness** in this selection process. When T → 0 (greedy decoding), the model always picks the word with the highest probability — the output becomes completely deterministic. The same prompt produces exactly the same text every time. When T = 1, the model samples from the original probability distribution — high-probability words are more likely to be chosen, but low-probability words can occasionally surface. When T > 1, the probability distribution is "flattened" — rare words are artificially boosted, and the output becomes more unpredictable.

---

这恰好是第三章"模糊性是联想和创造的来源"这一论断的数学实现。T = 0 时，LLM 退化成一个确定性的函数——它永远说最"安全"的话，但也永远不会有出人意料的联想、隐喻或幽默。T 升高时，模型开始在语义空间中"走梯度"——它偶尔选择一个低概率但相关联的词，然后从这个词出发继续生成，从而走进了一条全新的语义路径。**随机的采样噪声，是 LLM 创造力的物理基底。**

This is precisely the mathematical realization of Chapter 3's claim that "ambiguity is the source of association and creativity." At T = 0, the LLM degenerates into a deterministic function — it always says the "safest" thing, but it will never produce a surprising association, metaphor, or joke. As T rises, the model begins to "walk the gradient" in semantic space — it occasionally picks a low-probability but semantically related word, then continues generating from that word, thus walking into an entirely new semantic path. **The noise of random sampling is the physical substrate of LLM creativity.**

---

但随机性也有它的代价。T > 0 时，LLM 开始产生"幻觉"——编造不存在的事实、论文或函数 API。幻觉不是模型的 bug——它是随机采样在事实准确性这个维度上必然产生的副作用。T = 0 可以消灭绝大部分幻觉，但也同时消灭了所有的意外和创造。**T 是确定性与随机性之间的一个旋钮——调左，获得安全和无聊；调右，获得创造和危险。**

But randomness has its cost. At T > 0, LLMs begin to "hallucinate" — fabricating non-existent facts, papers, or function APIs. Hallucination is not a bug of the model — it is the inevitable side effect of random sampling on the dimension of factual accuracy. T = 0 can eliminate most hallucinations, but it also eliminates all surprise and creativity. **T is a knob between determinism and randomness — turn it left, you get safety and boredom; turn it right, you get creativity and danger.**

---

由此可以重新理解第三章结尾的受控自然语言：**CNL 的设计，不仅关乎词汇和语法，还关乎"随机性处方"。** 对于"生成一段代码"这种任务，T 应该接近 0——你需要确定性地翻译需求。对于"头脑风暴这个功能的各种设计可能"，T 可以更高——你需要随机性在语义空间中帮你探索。一个好的 CNL，可能需要在 prompt 中同时指定"说什么"（语言约束）和"怎么说"（温度约束）。这两者加在一起，才构成了完整的"LLM 编程语言"。

This provides a fresh understanding of the controlled natural language discussed at the end of Chapter 3: **CNL design is not only about vocabulary and grammar, but also about the "prescription of randomness."** For a task like "generate this piece of code," T should be near 0 — you need deterministic translation of requirements. For "brainstorm possible designs for this feature," T can be higher — you need randomness to help explore semantic space. A good CNL may need to simultaneously specify in the prompt both "what to say" (language constraints) and "how to say it" (temperature constraints). Together, these two constitute the complete "LLM programming language."

## 确定性与可验证性

## Determinism and Verifiability

确定性的程序有一个被低估的美德：可复现性。一个确定性的排序算法如果出错，你运行一次就看到了。一个随机化的排序算法——比如在百万分之一的情况下会出错——你可能运行一千次也撞不到那个错误。Fuzzing（随机模糊测试）正是利用了随机性来探索程序输入空间中那些确定性的测试不会去碰的角落。但它永远无法给你"这个程序是正确的"的证明——它只能给你"在 N 次随机测试中，没有发现错误"的概率信心。

Deterministic programs have an undervalued virtue: reproducibility. A deterministic sorting algorithm that has a bug — you run it once, and you see it. A randomized sorting algorithm that fails once in a million runs — you might run it a thousand times and never hit that failure. Fuzzing (random fuzz testing) exploits randomness precisely to explore corners of the program input space that deterministic tests would never touch. But it can never give you a proof that "this program is correct" — it can only give you probabilistic confidence that "in N random tests, no errors were found."

---

这就引入了计算科学中永恒的张力：**随机性给了我们覆盖广度（在概率上），但拿走了确定性的保证。** 形式化验证（formal verification）站在这个光谱的另一端——它试图用逻辑推导给出一个数学证明，保证程序在所有可能的输入下都满足规约。但形式化验证的代价是指数级的人力成本。大多数实用系统生存在这两者之间：用随机测试推进覆盖面，用类型系统和静态分析守住底线。

This introduces an eternal tension in computing science: **randomness gives us breadth of coverage (probabilistically) but takes away deterministic guarantees.** Formal verification stands at the other end of this spectrum — it attempts to give a mathematical proof, through logical derivation, that a program satisfies its specification for all possible inputs. But the cost of formal verification is enormous human effort. Most practical systems live somewhere in between: using random testing to push coverage boundaries, and using type systems and static analysis to hold the baseline.

## 本章小结

## Chapter Summary

确定性与随机性，不是"正确"与"错误"的对立。它们是计算科学中的**两种货币**，各自有其汇率和适用场景：

Determinism and randomness are not "right" versus "wrong." They are **two currencies** in computing science, each with its own exchange rate and domain of applicability:

1. **随机性是信息的水源。** 没有随机性，香农熵为零——信息量这个概念本身就不存在。Randomness is the wellspring of information. Without randomness, Shannon entropy is zero — the very concept of information quantity would not exist.

2. **随机性是一种计算资源。** 它可以被主动注入算法，在"确定性正确"和"计算成本"之间做 tradeoff。蒙特卡洛在高维空间中比确定性方法更高效——这不是妥协，是数学事实。Randomness is a computational resource. It can be actively injected into algorithms to trade off between "deterministic correctness" and "computational cost." Monte Carlo is more efficient than deterministic methods in high dimensions — this is not a compromise; it is a mathematical fact.

3. **"随机"是相对观察者的。** 伪随机序列对不知道种子的人不可预测，对知道种子的人完全确定。随机性与第三章的"黑话"同构——它取决于你是否拥有解码密钥。"Random" is relative to the observer. A pseudo-random sequence is unpredictable to those without the seed, yet completely deterministic to those with it. Randomness is isomorphic to Chapter 3's "jargon" — it depends on whether you hold the decoding key.

4. **温度是"模糊性剂量"的旋钮。** LLM 的 T 参数让你可以在确定的安全和随机的创造之间连续调节。T = 0 消灭幻觉也消灭创意；T > 0 带来创造力也带来危险。这是第三章"模糊性是创造的来源"的数学实现。Temperature is the knob for "dosage of ambiguity." The T parameter of LLMs allows continuous tuning between deterministic safety and random creativity. T = 0 eliminates hallucinations but also creativity; T > 0 brings creativity but also danger. This is the mathematical realization of Chapter 3's "ambiguity is the source of creativity."

5. **确定性与可验证性是相关的。** 确定性系统可以给出"证明"；随机系统只能给出"概率信心"。实用计算生存在这两者之间。Determinism and verifiability are a pair. Deterministic systems can yield "proofs"; random systems can only yield "probabilistic confidence." Practical computing lives in between.
