## Exercise: Kepler's Lifetime FLOPS

Johannes Kepler（1571–1630）花了大约八年时间——从 1601 到 1609——用 Tycho Brahe 的火星观测数据推导出了行星运动的三大定律（其中的前两条发表在 *Astronomia Nova* 中）。这是一项**纯粹的手工数值计算**：没有微积分（牛顿还没出生），没有对数表（Napier 的对数在 Kepler 工作的晚期才出现），只有纸、笔、三角函数表和一台缓慢运转的人脑。他反复迭代——假设一个轨道形状，计算预测位置，和观测数据对比，调整参数，再来一次——直到残差从几度收敛到几个角分。

Johannes Kepler (1571–1630) spent roughly eight years — from 1601 to 1609 — deriving the first two of his three laws of planetary motion from Tycho Brahe's Mars observational data, published in *Astronomia Nova.* This was **pure manual numerical computation:** no calculus (Newton wasn't born yet), no logarithms (Napier's logarithms appeared only late in Kepler's career), only paper, pen, trigonometric tables, and one slow human brain. He iterated — assume an orbit shape, compute predicted positions, compare against observational data, adjust parameters, repeat — until the residuals shrank from several degrees to a few arcminutes.

**任务：估计 Kepler 一生完成了多少次浮点运算（FLOPS），用 Fermi 问题的思路。**

**Task: Estimate how many floating-point operations (FLOPS) Kepler completed in his lifetime, approaching it as a Fermi problem.**

一种可能的推演：

- Kepler 活了 59 岁。假设他真正从事高强度计算的"活跃计算期"大约是 30 年（从 25 岁到 55 岁）。

- 一小时能算多少次？1600 年的数学家做一次乘法——比如把一个六位观测数据乘以一个三角函数值——大概需要几十秒到一分钟：查三角函数表要翻页，乘法要手写竖式，加法要进位。假设一次"浮点运算等价操作"（乘或除，或者一次三角函数查表并插值）需要大约 30 秒。那一小时大约是 120 次运算。

One possible chain of reasoning:

- Kepler lived to 59. Assume his "active computation period" spanned roughly 30 years (from age 25 to 55).
- Assume he computed 300 days per year (deducting illness, travel, correspondence, publishing chores).
- Assume 10 hours of computation per day (actual time hunched over numerical work — not reading theory, not writing prose, not wrangling patrons).
- How many operations per hour? A mathematician in the 1600s doing a multiplication — say, a six-digit observational value times a trigonometric function — would need tens of seconds to a minute: flipping through trig tables, writing out long multiplication, carrying digits. Assume one "FLOP-equivalent operation" (a multiply or divide, or a trig table lookup with interpolation) takes about 30 seconds. That's roughly 120 operations per hour.

$$
30 \text{ 年} \times 300 \text{ 天/年} \times 10 \text{ 小时/天} \times 120 \text{ 次/小时} \approx 1.08 \times 10^7 \text{ 次运算}

$$
30 \text{ years} \times 300 \text{ days/year} \times 10 \text{ hours/day} \times 120 \text{ operations/hour} \approx 1.08 \times 10^7 \text{ operations}
$$

**Roughly ten million operations. About 10 megaFLOPs — not a rate, a lifetime total. The entire computational output of Johannes Kepler's life.**

A smartphone SoC from the 2020s peaks at roughly 10¹² FLOPS (1 teraFLOP/s). In a single second, it completes roughly the computational equivalent of ten Keplers' ten Keplers' ten Keplers' ten Keplers' lifetime output — and Kepler was the most ferocious "human computer" of his era. **This is what asymptotics means on the scale of a single human lifespan.** Not "computers are faster than humans" — but "the growth of computational scale washes away the most extreme human outlier into a chasm that can only be expressed in exponential notation."

这个计算的要点不是"得到一个准确的数字"——是让你**感受一个数量级**：人类大脑——即使是 Kepler 这种级别的——在纯粹比特翻转能力上，和硅基计算之间的差距，不是"差的很多"，是"本质不在同一个物理范畴里"。

The point of this calculation is not "getting an accurate number" — it is **feeling an order of magnitude:** the gap between a human brain — even one of Kepler's caliber — and silicon computation in raw bit-flipping capacity is not "a large difference." The two are not in the same physical category.

## 讨论：计算是科学的第零范式——重排四个范式的真实顺序

## Discussion: Computation as the Zeroth Paradigm — Rearranging the True Order of the Four Paradigms

Jim Gray 在 2007 年提出了一个影响深远的叙事——"科学的第四范式"：

1. **经验科学**（千年前）：观察自然，描述现象。
2. **理论科学**（几百年前）：建立模型，推导定律。
3. **计算科学**（几十年前）：模拟复杂系统。
4. **数据密集型科学**（现在）：从数据里直接发现模式——不需要先验模型。

Jim Gray proposed an influential narrative in 2007 — the "Fourth Paradigm of Science":

1. **Empirical science** (thousands of years ago): observe nature, describe phenomena.
2. **Theoretical science** (hundreds of years ago): build models, derive laws.
3. **Computational science** (decades ago): simulate complex systems.
4. **Data-intensive science** (now): discover patterns directly from data — no prior model needed.

这个叙事结构优雅——但它包含一个不是"局限"、而是**时序错误**的根本问题。纠正它之后，四个范式的编号需要重新分配。

This narrative is structurally elegant — but it contains a problem that is not a "limitation" but a **chronological error.** Once corrected, the numbering of the four paradigms must be reassigned.

**计算不是第三范式。计算是第零范式。**

**Computation is not the third paradigm. It is the zeroth paradigm.**

### Kepler 的顺序

### Kepler's Order

回到这个练习最核心的事实——我们刚才有意让你在算 FLOPS 的时候自己发现它——不是 Kepler 算得有多快或多慢，而是**他的计算和他的理论之间的时序关系**：

Return to the central fact of this exercise — the one we deliberately let you discover while computing the FLOPS — which is not how fast or slow Kepler computed, but **the chronological relationship between his computation and his theory:**

**Kepler 先算了八年。然后才有了行星运动定律。**

**Kepler computed for eight years first. Then came the laws of planetary motion.**

这八年不是"验证已有理论"的计算。这八年不是"模拟已知模型"的计算。这八年的计算本身就是**理论的生成过程**。Kepler 不知道答案——他只有一个方向：残差还有几个角分，轨道还不是椭圆。他迭代——计算，对比，调整，再计算，再对比——直到残差收敛到一个他无法继续忽略的形状。那个形状就是椭圆。**理论不是输入——理论是计算的收敛结果。**

Those eight years were not computation to "verify an existing theory." They were not computation to "simulate a known model." Those eight years of computation constituted the **generative process of the theory itself.** Kepler didn't know the answer — he only had a direction: the residuals were still several arcminutes, the orbit wasn't yet an ellipse. He iterated — compute, compare, adjust, recompute, compare again — until the residuals converged to a shape he could no longer ignore. That shape was the ellipse. **Theory was not the input — theory was the convergence product of computation.**

如果将这个顺序画成流程图，四个范式的叙事是：

If we draw this as a flow chart, the four-paradigm narrative is:

```
观察 → 理论 → 计算 → 大数据
Observation → Theory → Computation → Big Data
```

但 Kepler 的真实工序是：

But Kepler's actual workflow was:

```
数据 + 计算 → 残差收敛 → 理论
Data + Computation → Residual Convergence → Theory
```

计算在理论**之前**。计算是理论得以从中析出的溶液。

Computation precedes theory. Computation is the solution from which theory precipitates.

### 这不是 Kepler 独有的工作方式

### This Is Not Unique to Kepler

- **Galileo** 没有先提出落体定律然后做实验。他用斜面反复计时、测量、制表，让数据把匀加速这个模式**逼出来**。测量和计算在前，定律在后。

- **Galileo** did not first propose the law of falling bodies and then experiment. He repeatedly timed, measured, and tabulated on inclined planes, letting the data **force out** the pattern of uniform acceleration. Measurement and computation came first; the law came after.

- **Newton** 发明微积分——一种纯计算工具——**为了**做力学。不是先有力学后有微积分。微积分是计算万有引力定律必需的运算语言。计算工具是理论的前提条件，不是理论之后的便利品。

- **Newton** invented calculus — a purely computational tool — **in order to** do mechanics. Mechanics did not precede calculus. Calculus was the operational language required to compute the law of universal gravitation. The computational tool was a prerequisite for the theory, not a convenience added afterward.

- **Maxwell** 花了十年计算场线与通量，才把结果压缩成今天我们看到的四个向量方程。他的电磁理论——那个被称作"物理学第二伟大的统一"的理论——不是从方程推导出来的。方程是从计算场线中**压缩**出来的。

- **Maxwell** spent a decade computing field lines and fluxes before compressing the results into the four vector equations we recognize today. His electromagnetic theory — hailed as "the second great unification of physics" — was not derived from the equations. The equations were **compressed** out of computing field lines.

- **Einstein** 在 1905 年的三篇论文之前，追着 Maxwell 方程和相对性原理算了十年。Lorentz 变换——狭义相对论的数学核心——在 Einstein 把它解释为时空结构之前，已经在 Lorentz 和 Poincaré 的计算中被反复使用和改写。**方程先被算出来，然后才被理解。**

- **Einstein**, before his three 1905 papers, spent a decade chasing the implications of Maxwell's equations and the relativity principle through calculation. The Lorentz transformation — the mathematical core of special relativity — had been repeatedly used and reformulated in the computations of Lorentz and Poincaré before Einstein interpreted it as the structure of spacetime. **The equations were computed first, then understood.**

这不是"几个伟大科学家的巧合"。这是**科学的默认工作模式**：你在黑暗中算。残差给你方向。迭代足够多次之后，模式凝成结构。你给结构起一个名字——"定律""方程""定理"——但那已经是计算之后的事了。

This is not "a coincidence among a few great scientists." It is the **default operating mode of science:** you compute in the dark. The residuals give you direction. After enough iterations, a pattern condenses into structure. You give the structure a name — "law," "equation," "theorem" — but that is already after the computation.

### 重排

### Reorder

如果非要给科学的范式编号，正确的顺序是：

If one must number the paradigms of science, the correct order is:

**第零范式：计算。** 收集数据，做数值运算，发现残差，迭代，让模式从计算中被逼出来。这是科学真正开始的地方——在任何一个理论被说出来之前。Kepler 的八年。Galileo 的斜面。Newton 的瘟疫年月。**计算是科学的原生汤。**

**Zeroth Paradigm: Computation.** Collect data, perform numerical operations, discover residuals, iterate, let patterns be forced out of the computation. This is where science actually begins — before any theory is articulated. Kepler's eight years. Galileo's inclined planes. Newton's plague years. **Computation is the primordial soup of science.**

**第一范式：理论。** 将计算中反复出现的模式压缩成简洁的因果陈述——定律、方程、公理。这是计算的**输出**，不是计算的输入。

**First Paradigm: Theory.** Compress the patterns that repeatedly emerged from computation into concise causal statements — laws, equations, axioms. This is the **output** of computation, not its input.

**第二范式：实验。** 用理论推导出的推论设计新的观测——去测量理论说你应该去测量的东西。理论告诉你该看哪里；实验验证你看到的是否正确。

**Second Paradigm: Experiment.** Use the deductions of theory to design new observations — to measure what theory says you should measure. Theory tells you where to look; experiment verifies whether what you see is correct.

**第三范式：大规模模拟。** 当一个系统的理论已知但解析上不可解时——用第零范式的方法在更快的计算基底上复现它。这是计算在另一个尺度上的递归。

**Third Paradigm: Large-scale Simulation.** When a system's theory is known but analytically intractable — reproduce it on faster computational substrates using the methods of the zeroth paradigm. This is computation recurring at another scale.

**（所谓的）第四范式：计算驱动发现。** 用机器做 Kepler 用手做的事——从数据中让理论自发地**结晶**。但这根本不是"新的"。这是第零范式的回归——用硅而不是用神经元。

**(So-called) Fourth Paradigm: Computation-driven Discovery.** Use machines to do what Kepler did by hand — let theory spontaneously **crystallize** from data. But this is not "new" at all. It is the return of the zeroth paradigm — with silicon instead of neurons.

### 这个重排不是文字游戏

### This Reordering Is Not a Word Game

把计算从第三格移到第零格，改变了你对以下三件事的理解：

Moving computation from slot three to slot zero changes how you understand three things:

**1. 计算与理论的关系。** 在四范式叙事里，计算是理论的仆人——你有了模型之后，用计算去跑模拟。但在真实工序里，**理论是计算的压缩产物。** 当你训练一个神经网络让它拟合数据时，本质上你在重复 Kepler 做过的事——你让残差在参数空间中收敛到一个你尚未命名的形状。那个形状就是"理论"——无论你最终是否用人类能读的语言写下来。

**1. The relationship between computation and theory.** In the four-paradigm narrative, computation is the servant of theory — once you have a model, you use computation to run simulations. But in the real workflow, **theory is a compressed product of computation.** When you train a neural network to fit data, you are in essence repeating what Kepler did — you are letting residuals converge in parameter space toward a shape you haven't yet named. That shape is "theory" — whether or not you ultimately write it down in a language humans can read.

**2. "第四范式"的实质。** Kepler 用 30 年做了 ∼10 megaFLOPs。你的一张 GPU 一秒做 ∼10¹² FLOPS。这不是"范式的转变"——这是**同一个范式在不同计算基底上的展开。** 把它叫"第四范式"是在混淆基底的算力差异和活动本质的差异。范式之变，改变的是**你在做什么**；基底之变，改变的只是**你用什么做**。计算驱动的科学发现并不因为基底从神经元变成了硅就变成了"新范式"——正如 Kepler 的第三定律并不因为他从手算椭圆换成了查对数表就变成了另一条定律。

**2. The substance of the "fourth paradigm."** Kepler achieved ∼10 megaFLOPs in 30 years. Your GPU achieves ∼10¹² FLOPS in one second. This is not a "paradigm shift" — it is **the same paradigm unfolding on a different computational substrate.** Calling it the "fourth paradigm" conflates a difference in substrate throughput with a difference in the nature of the activity. A paradigm shift changes **what you are doing**; a substrate shift changes only **what you are doing it with.** Computation-driven scientific discovery does not become a "new paradigm" just because the substrate switched from neurons to silicon — any more than Kepler's Third Law became a different law when he switched from hand-multiplying ellipses to looking up logarithms.

**3. 估计和渐进是第零范式的元工具。** 本章讲的这两件事——估计和渐进分析——正是你用来判断"在当前计算基底上，哪些模式能从噪声中突围"的工具。它们不是哪个具体学科的工具。它们是**第零范式的操作手册**——无论你用的计算基底是 Kepler 的笔还是一个 TPU pod。

**3. Estimation and asymptotics are the meta-tools of the zeroth paradigm.** The two things this chapter teaches — estimation and asymptotic analysis — are precisely the tools you use to judge "on the current computational substrate, which patterns can break out of the noise." They are not tools of any specific discipline. They are the **operating manual of the zeroth paradigm** — whether your computational substrate is Kepler's pen or a TPU pod.

**结论：四个范式的叙事真正的反讽，不在于它夸大了计算的作用——而在于它把计算放错了位置。** 它不是第四个登场的角色。它是舞台本身。

**Conclusion: The true irony of the four-paradigm narrative is not that it exaggerates the role of computation — but that it misplaces computation.** It is not the fourth character to walk on stage. It is the stage itself.

---

这一错位有一个更深层的成因。自印刷时代以来，科学的**出版形式**——论文——只能容纳"理论推演"这个最终阶段的产物。Kepler 可以把 *Astronomia Nova* 里完成形态的定律、几何论证和表格印在纸上，但他无法把——也没有语言可以描述——那八年里他在桌前逐行计算、逐次调整、逐度收敛的**过程**出版出来。Newton 的 *Principia* 以公理—命题—证明的欧几里得形式呈现，读者看不到瘟疫年月里他独自在羊毛索普做的微积分计算。印刷机的物理约束——页码、排版、线性的论证结构——要求科学以**哲学论文的形式**呈现：以第一原则为起点，以逻辑推演为路径，以定律为终点。它无法以"我先算了这些，然后那些残差变小了，然后我猜了一个椭圆"的形式呈现。

There is a deeper cause for this misplacement. Since the print era, science's **publication form** — the paper — can only accommodate the final-stage product of "theoretical deduction." Kepler could print the finished laws, geometric proofs, and tables of *Astronomia Nova* on paper, but he could not — and had no language to — publish the **process** of those eight years: the line-by-line computation, adjustment after adjustment, degree-by-degree convergence at his desk. Newton's *Principia* was presented in the Euclidean form of axioms–propositions–proofs; the reader never saw the calculus he performed alone in Woolsthorpe during the plague years. The physical constraints of the printing press — page counts, typesetting, the linear structure of argument — demanded that science be presented as **philosophical treatise:** first principles as the starting point, logical deduction as the path, laws as the endpoint. It could not be presented as "I computed these first, then the residuals got smaller, then I guessed an ellipse."

这种出版媒介的偏压——它不能在物理上记录计算的泥泞过程，只能记录理论的结晶形态——在过去四百年里塑造了我们对"什么是科学"的集体认知：**理论推演就是科学的全部。** 计算被压到了论文的附录、脚注、或者干脆被省略的数字表格里——不可见，不可引，不被承认为科学活动的主体。四个范式的叙事不过是这个四百年错觉的一次晚近回声——把计算排到第三位，不是因为它真的在理论之后发生，而是因为它在**出版的可见性排序**中排在最后。

This bias of the publication medium — its physical inability to record the muddy process of computation, only the crystallized form of theory — has shaped our collective understanding of "what science is" over the past four hundred years: **theoretical deduction is the whole of science.** Computation was squeezed into appendices, footnotes, or numerical tables omitted altogether — invisible, uncitable, unrecognized as the main body of scientific activity. The four-paradigm narrative is merely a late echo of this four-hundred-year illusion — ranking computation third, not because it actually happens after theory, but because it ranks last in the **visibility order of publication.**
